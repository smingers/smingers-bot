**Factors supporting eventual US crossover (before 2200):**

**Factors against US crossover:**

1. **No published projection shows crossover before 2100:** Even IHME's uncertainty intervals barely touch crossover at extreme tails

2. **Projection uncertainty increases dramatically beyond 2100:**
   - 174-year forecasts are inherently unreliable
   - Major unpredictable events likely: wars, pandemics, climate migration, technological changes (AI, longevity breakthroughs)
   - Policy changes could reverse trends (China could liberalize immigration; US could restrict it)

3. **Fertility could rebound:** While no East Asian country has shown rebound, South Korea/Japan have only been at ultra-low fertility for 20-30 years. Over 174 years, cultural/economic/technological changes could shift preferences

4. **Current gap remains large:** Even in 2100, China is projected at 1.5-2x US population. Closing remaining gap requires another 50-100 years of trend persistence

5. **Base rate of extreme long-term forecast accuracy:** Very low. The world of 2200 will likely be as different from 2026 as 2026 is from 1850

**Integrating factors:**

The question asks about "any point before 2200" - a disjunctive probability across 174 years. This significantly increases the probability versus asking about a specific year.

Key insight: All authoritative projections show the gap narrowing to ~1.5:1 by 2100, with trends pointing toward eventual crossover if continued. The Perplexity report suggests "crossover perhaps in the 2110-2150 window" based on straightforward extrapolation.

The question becomes: What is the probability that current trends persist long enough (roughly 100-125 years) for crossover, versus the probability that some combination of events/changes reverses the trend or makes projections meaningless?
