## Probability Calibration

The outside view established 32% based on:
- Strong directional trend through 2100 (China declining, US stable/growing)
- Plausible extrapolation suggesting crossover in 2110-2150 window
- Massive uncertainty over 174-year horizon

The January 2026 information strengthens the case for crossover by:

2. Showing continued acceleration of decline despite policy interventions
3. Providing expert consensus that this is an unprecedented demographic crisis
4. Suggesting the crossover window may be 2130-2145 rather than 2150

However, the fundamental challenge remains: forecasting 174 years into the future with any confidence is inherently problematic. The world of 2200 will be as alien to us as 2026 would be to someone in 1850.

The adjustment from 32% to 41% reflects:
- High confidence (85%+) that current trends continue through 2100
- Moderate confidence (50-60%) that trends continue 2100-2150
- Moderate-high confidence (60-70%) that crossover occurs if trends continue to 2150
- Low confidence (30-40%) in any specific long-term scenario

**Key insight**: The question asks for "at any point before 2200," which is a disjunctive probability. This significantly increases the probability versus asking about a specific year or sustained crossover. A temporary crossover due to a catastrophic event in China (pandemic, war, climate disaster) would count, even if the long-term trend later reverses.

The 41% probability reflects:
- ~5% chance of crossover by 2100 (up from 3% due to accelerated Chinese decline)
- ~30% chance of crossover 2100-2175 (up from 25% due to earlier crossover window)
- ~6% chance of crossover 2175-2200 (up from 4% due to additional event-driven scenarios)

This calibration acknowledges that current evidence strengthens the case for eventual crossover while maintaining appropriate humility about the extreme uncertainty inherent in forecasting nearly two centuries into the future.
