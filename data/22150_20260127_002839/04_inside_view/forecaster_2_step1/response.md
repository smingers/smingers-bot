## (d) Justification for Outside View

**Baseline Scenario (No crossover before 2100):**
All three authoritative sources (UN WPP 2024, Census IDB 2024, IHME 2020) agree China remains larger through 2100, with ratios of 1.5:1 to 2.2:1. This represents 74 of the 174 years—42% of the timeframe.

**Post-2100 Extrapolation (2100-2200):**
- **Momentum factors favoring crossover:**
  1. China's TFR (1.0-1.18) far below replacement; historical evidence shows reversal nearly impossible once this low
  2. USA's sustained immigration (~1M/year) adds ~100M by 2100, likely continues
  3. China's working-age population already shrinking, accelerating dependency burden
  4. No Chinese policy success in raising fertility (2015 two-child, 2021 three-child policies failed)

- **Momentum factors against crossover:**
  1. Massive initial gap: China 633M vs USA 421M in 2100 (212M difference)
  2. USA fertility also below replacement (1.62); requires immigration to grow
  3. Immigration policies highly uncertain over 174-year horizon
  4. China could implement massive immigration policy shift (unlikely but possible)
  5. Fertility could partially rebound in China as economic pressures ease

**Quantitative Anchoring:**
- IHME's 95% UI barely touches crossover at extreme tails by 2100
- If China continues at TFR ~1.0 and USA at TFR ~1.6 + immigration, simple exponential models suggest crossover around 2120-2150
- This gives ~94-124 years to crossover, or ~54-71% of remaining timeframe after 2100

**Uncertainty Considerations:**
- 174-year projections are extraordinarily uncertain (no official models exist)
- Black swans: wars, pandemics, climate migration, technological fertility breakthroughs
- Policy shifts: China could adopt mass immigration; USA could close borders
- However, demographic momentum is powerful—populations are "tankers, not speedboats"

**Integration:**
The outside view suggests crossover is plausible but not certain. Given:
- 0% chance before 2100 (all sources agree)
- Moderate-to-good chance 2100-2200 if current trends persist
- Significant uncertainty from 174-year horizon
- Reference class shows ultra-low fertility rarely reverses

A reasonable outside view places probability in the 35-55% range, acknowledging both the powerful momentum toward crossover and the profound uncertainties of century-plus projections.

2. **Conditional probability post-2100:** Given no crossover by 2100 (98.5% scenario), what's probability of crossover in subsequent century?

   - If trends shift (fertility rebounds, immigration changes): Lower probability, perhaps 20-30%

3. **Combined probability:** 
   - P(crossover) = P(by 2100) + P(after 2100 | no crossover by 2100) × P(no crossover by 2100)
