The consensus narrative is dangerously clean: "Bowman will be the sole dissenter." This is exactly the kind of story that sounds authoritative but often misses reality. Here's what the consensus might be missing:

3. **Pre-meeting predictions are notoriously unreliable**: Members' public positioning before meetings doesn't always match their actual votes. Bowman might ultimately vote with the majority.

4. **The "no other dissenters mentioned" problem**: Just because analysts don't predict other dissenters doesn't mean they won't appear. Surprises happen.

5. **Base rate wisdom**: 74% of meetings have zero dissents for a reason - consensus-building is the Fed's norm. We need strong evidence to deviate significantly from this.
