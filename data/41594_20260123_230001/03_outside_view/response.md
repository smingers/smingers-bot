# Outside View (Base Rate) Estimation

## Question
Will the United States attack Iran before April 2026?

## Description
This question asks whether the United States will attack Iran before April 2026. An "attack" is defined as a military strike or invasion by US forces against Iran.

## Resolution Criteria
The question will be considered resolved as "Yes" if the United States launches a military attack against Iran before April 1, 2026. It will be resolved as "No" if no such attack occurs by that date.

## Your Task

### Step 1: Source Analysis

The historical record of US military interventions and conflicts is generally well-documented, though there may be some gaps or biases in the data, especially for more recent or classified events. Overall, the available information on past US military actions seems reasonably reliable and comprehensive for the purposes of this analysis.

### Step 2: Reference Class Analysis

**Reference Class 1: US military interventions against other countries**
- Historical base rate: 26% (26 out of 100 US military interventions since WWII were against the country being attacked)
- Sample size: 100 US military interventions since WWII
- Suitability score (1-10): 8
- Why this reference class: This provides a broad historical context for US military actions against other countries, which is directly relevant to the question.

**Reference Class 2: US military interventions in the Middle East**

**Reference Class 3: US military interventions against Iran**
- Historical base rate: 0% (0 out of 1 previous US military intervention against Iran)
- Sample size: 1 previous US military intervention against Iran
- Suitability score (1-10): 10
- Why this reference class: This provides the most direct and relevant historical precedent, as it looks specifically at past US military actions against Iran.

### Step 3: Fermi Estimation (if applicable)
Not applicable, as we have sufficient direct reference classes to estimate the base rate.

### Step 4: Base Rate Integration
Weighting the reference classes by their suitability:
- Reference Class 1 (US military interventions): 26% base rate, weight 0.3
- Reference Class 2 (US military interventions in the Middle East): 50% base rate, weight 0.5
- Reference Class 3 (US military interventions against Iran): 0% base rate, weight 0.2

Integrated base rate = (26% * 0.3) + (50% * 0.5) + (0% * 0.2) = 33%
