## Step 6: Final Reasoning

As a synthesizer integrating multiple perspectives, several key factors emerge:

**Critical Upward Pressures:**
1. **Concrete military positioning**: Unlike mere rhetoric, actual carrier strike groups and fighter deployments represent genuine capability in theater. While often used for deterrence, they also enable rapid action if ordered.

2. **Compressed timeframe**: We're forecasting only 10 weeks out during an active crisis. The base rate covers 33+ years of varying conditions; concentrating risk in this specific window justifies significant upward adjustment.

3. **Conditional commitment**: The US President has publicly stated conditions for military action (continued killing of protesters). These conditions appear to be continuing, creating a commitment trap where inaction could damage credibility.

4. **Volatile environment**: With massive protests, economic collapse, and both sides on military alert, the risk of miscalculation, accidents, or provocations is elevated.

**Critical Downward Pressures:**
1. **Historical pattern**: US threats against Iran have rarely materialized into direct attacks on Iranian territory. The base rate of ~7% reflects this restraint.

2. **Costs of action**: A US attack on Iran would have massive regional and global consequences. Even with deployments, decision-makers face enormous disincentives.

3. **Deterrence success**: The very presence of forces and threats may be achieving their purpose (deterring Iranian actions) without requiring actual use.

4. **Allied concerns**: European allies' opposition to escalation provides constraint on US action.

**Synthesis:**
The current situation represents a genuinely elevated risk period compared to the historical baseline. The convergence of active military deployments, explicit threats, internal Iranian instability, and a short forecasting window creates multiple pathways to conflict. However, the strong historical pattern of restraint, enormous costs of action, and effectiveness of deterrence suggest the probability remains well below 50%.

The evidence clearly points UPWARD from the 7% base rate, but not dramatically so. A roughly 3x increase (to ~21%) reflects the acute nature of the current crisis while respecting the powerful forces that have historically prevented US-Iran direct military conflict.
