## Step 3: Direction of Update

### Contrarian Perspective: What Is Everyone Missing?

**The Consensus Blind Spot:**

1. **Trump Unpredictability Premium**: Historical base rates assume rational actor models. Trump's documented pattern of impulsive decisions isn't fully captured in Cold War-era precedents.

2. **Distraction as Motivation, Not Constraint**: The consensus views multiple crises as constraints. Contrarian view: A president facing impeachment threats and political "bloodbath" might see a "rally around the flag" military action as salvation, not additional burden.

3. **Limited Strike vs. Full War**: The question asks about "attack," not "invasion." A limited strike (similar to 2020 Soleimani assassination) requires far less preparation than the base rate suggests. This could happen with minimal warning.

4. **Information Blackout**: Absence of evidence isn't evidence of absence. Real operational planning would be classified. The lack of public indicators might reflect successful operational security, not lack of planning.

**However, Contrarian Cuts Both Ways:**

5. **The Billboard Anomaly**: This article is highly suspicious. If fabricated or misattributed, it suggests the research quality is poor, making the entire evidence base unreliable.

6. **Diplomatic Engagement Overlooked**: Macron's active diplomacy ("we can do great things on Iran") suggests backchannels are open. This contradicts imminent attack scenarios.

7. **The April Deadline**: Only 9-10 weeks away. Even a limited strike requires:
   - Intelligence preparation
   - Asset positioning
   - Legal framework (AUMF or Article II justification)
   - Allied notification
   None of this appears in evidence.
