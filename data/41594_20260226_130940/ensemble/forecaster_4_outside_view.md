But how often is a “crisis clearly under way”? Over 46 years (1979-2025) I count ~20 three-month blocks that fit that description; strikes happened in 7 of them → base P(strike | any random 3-mo block) ≈ 7/20 ≈ 0.35, and within 30 d ≈ 0.25.  

d) Justification  
1. The current moment clearly qualifies as a crisis: large anti-regime protests, US presidential threats of intervention, continued Israeli-Iranian skirmishing.  
2. The US struck Iranian soil only eight months ago, showing willingness and capability. A follow-on strike would therefore not require fresh domestic “proof-of-concept”.  
3. Trump’s statements (“time for new leadership”) raise the rhetorical temperature but so far there is no open carrier strike group inside the Gulf, no reported evacuation order for US nationals, and no new UNSC consultations – all signals that normally precede kinetic action.  
4. The domestic US political cost of a limited strike is historically low; presidents from both parties have carried out such actions without congressional authorisation roughly once every two years.  
5. Counter-pressure: the Iranian regime is already under severe strain; another overt US attack could unite nationalist sentiment and undercut the protesters – a strategic downside the US military and NSC are aware of.  
6. Expert/market anchor: Sentinel’s crowd puts the chance at 59 %. Crowds tend to run “hot” in fresh crises; they were high by 15-20 percentage points in Syria 2018 and North Korea 2017 compared with outcomes.
