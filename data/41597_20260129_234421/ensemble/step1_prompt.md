   - 139 publicly traded banks had negative abnormal stock returns
   - Distribution skewed toward negative returns

- **Default Risk Perception:** The cost of insuring large banks against default jumped following the April 2025 tariff announcement, indicating investors perceived a higher risk of failure.

- **Market Recovery:** Financial markets dropped sharply following the April 2025 trade policy announcement but have since recovered.

- **Indirect Exposure Mechanism:** Banks have little direct exposure to trade but may be affected through: (1) lending to borrowers heavily reliant on trade or with extensive foreign supply chains, and (2) broader economic effects of trade policy on all borrowers.

The study used event study methodology examining abnormal stock returns around the April 2, 2025 tariff announcement, controlling for systematic market-wide movements using banks' beta relative to the S&P 500 index.
