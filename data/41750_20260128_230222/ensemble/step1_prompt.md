# Summary of Article

## Key Information Extracted:

**Objective Facts:**
- The BACEN SELIC rate is set by the Brazilian Central Bank (Banco Central do Brasil, also known as BACEN or BCB)
- The SELIC rate stands for "sistema especial de liquidacao e custodia"
- It is considered the most important interest rate of the Brazilian Central Bank
- The SELIC Rate is a benchmark/base rate used to influence interest rate levels in the Brazilian economy
- It is comparable to the Federal Reserve's Federal funds target rate or the ECB's refi rate
- The interest rate is calculated as an average of interbank interest rates charged for trade in government securities with a 1-day maturity
- Changes to the SELIC Rate affect interest rates for banking products (mortgages, savings, loans) and typically impact the exchange rate of the Brazilian Real

**Institutional Details:**
- The Banco Central do Brasil is responsible for monetary policy
- Its mission is to safeguard the stability of the national currency's buying power and the solvency and efficiency of the financial system
- The central bank can operate fully autonomously (unlike most other countries)
- The government monitors BACEN to check for irregularities
