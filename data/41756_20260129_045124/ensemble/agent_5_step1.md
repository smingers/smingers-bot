(b) Reference class analysis  

RC-1: U.S. escalation pattern when another country covertly arms an adversary (e.g., Iran supplying drones to Russia, North Korea supplying shells).  Tendency: initial intelligence warning → quiet diplomatic protest → public accusation within 6–18 months once evidence is solid → sanctions.  Hit rate ≈80%.  
RC-2: U.S. public naming-and-shaming of China on sensitive national-security issues (cyber-intrusions, balloon incident, South China Sea lasers).  In roughly two-thirds of cases, Washington eventually issues an unambiguous accusation once legal review is complete.  
RC-3 (control): U.S. warnings that never matured into accusations (e.g., Pakistan contemplating aiding the Taliban after Doha deal).  Roughly 50% fizzle.

RC-1 best matches the present problem: we have already moved through warning and sanctions stages; only the definitive public accusation box remains unchecked.
