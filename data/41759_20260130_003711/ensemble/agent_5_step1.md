b)  Reference-class analysis  
Candidates:  
• Short-horizon SBP policy-rate changes (≤ 4 months) since 2011 – 81 MPC windows.  
• Short-horizon EM central-bank changes with comparable inflation-targeting lite regimes (Turkey pre-2020, Egypt post-2017).

The first class is preferred: same institution, policy rule, domestic politics and IMF oversight.  From 2011-2025, the absolute change in the SBP rate over any 90-day window was:  
– zero 55 % of the time  
– ±25 bp 5 %  
– ±50 bp 22 %  
– ≥ 75 bp 18 % (all ≥ 9-month windows occurred during crises; none when inflation was inside target and the real rate already ≥ +3 %).  

Current context mirrors the “inside-target, positive-real-rate” subsample (2014-16 and parts of 2024-25).  In that subsample, 50 bp moves occurred 47 % of the time (always cuts), holds 46 %, other magnitudes 7 %.  No hikes were recorded.
