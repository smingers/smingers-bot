(b) Reference class analysis  
Candidates:  
1. SBP 3-month changes (2009-2025).  
2. Emerging-market central-bank 3-month changes when inflation is within target and IMF programme is in place.  
3. SBP behaviour in the last leg of previous easing cycles (e.g., 2013-14, mid-2020).  
The first class is the cleanest and uses an institutionally identical environment. From Jan 2009–Jan 2026 (171 months) there are 57 three-month periods; absolute change statistics:  
– No change: 31 % of periods  
– Cut ≤100 bp: 26 %  
– Cut 125-250 bp: 19 %  
– Cut ≥275 bp: 12 %  
– Hike: 12 %  
Because both inflation and the IMF require a positive real rate, tail-risk for hikes or very large cuts is lower than the unconditional series. Adjusted reference class chosen: SBP three-month moves when y/y CPI is 4-8 % and the real policy rate is ≥ 3 % (11 episodes). Outcomes: 45 % no-change, 45 % –50 bp or –100 bp, 9 % +50 bp.

(c) Timeframe analysis  
Days to resolution: 92 days (≈3.0 months). The MPC meets roughly every 6-8 weeks; two meetings (mid-Mar and late-Apr 2026) can affect the 1 May rate. Historically, the SBP seldom changes the rate between scheduled meetings.

(d) Justification (outside view)  
Status quo: 10.5 %. Real rate ≈ +4½ % (headline CPI 5-7 %). The 26 Jan MPS said the real rate is already “adequately positive” and cut banks’ CRR instead of the policy rate—signalling a preference to ease through liquidity first.  
Inflation outlook: base effects lift y/y CPI after March; SBP projects FY-26 average still 5-7 %. That reduces room for deep cuts before May but keeps a modest cut plausible.  
IMF programme: requires positive real rates; staff will hold the third EFF review mission in May 2026—another incentive for caution at the Apr MPC.  
Market & survey error: January surveys predicted a 50-100 bp cut and were wrong, implying the committee is more hawkish than consensus.  
Risk skew: Downside (cuts) outweigh upside (hikes) but the fat-tail on hikes persists (energy-price spike or rupee wobble).  

Synthesis: Most likely outcome is one 50 bp cut in either March or April, taking the rate to 10.0 %. Next-most-likely is no change (10.5 %). Larger moves need stronger data than seems likely in the next nine weeks; a surprise hike is a <1 in 10 shot.
