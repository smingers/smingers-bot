b. Reference-class analysis  
Candidates:  
i. Monthly seasonally-adjusted ABS unemployment rate, 1978-2025 (primary).  
ii. Same series over the last two post-COVID years (Jan 2023-Dec 2025).  
iii. Other OECD economies one-month moves.

Suitability: We want a one-month-ahead forecast.  The most relevant base rate is the empirical distribution of month-to-month changes in the ABS series (class ii) because structural conditions post-COVID differ from long-run history.  Over Jan 2023-Dec 2025 (36 observations) absolute monthly change averaged 0.11 pp, with 80 % of moves between –0.2 pp and +0.2 pp and max swing 0.4 pp.  I therefore adopt class ii.

c. Timeframe analysis  
Today → publication of February figure: 46 days.  Historically, two data prints occur in-between (January labour force on 19 Feb, February CPI partials).  Policy could change at 3 Feb RBA meeting, but even if cash rate rises 25 bp, labour demand reacts with a long lag.  Hence only modest drift is plausible within a month.

d. Justification for outside-view prediction  
• Starting point (last observed) = 4.1 % (Dec).  
• Forward indicators: SEEK ads –3.5 % over 2025 and down 1.2 % in Dec signal softening. NAB, ANZ job-ads series (not quoted but trending lower) corroborate.  
• Participation normally rises in February as students enter workforce; seasonals in ABS already adjust, but residual risk skewed slightly upward.  
• Consensus (TradingEconomics, CBA, etc.) expects Q1 average ≈4.3 %.  
• Month-to-month volatility small; however, sampling error of ABS estimate ≈±0.1 pp (one s.d.).  
Base-rate projection therefore centres a little above 4.1 %.  I place the median (50th-ish) at 4.2 – 4.3 %.  Tails: downside limited by record-low 3.4 % (Oct 2022) and recent uptick trend; upside tail capped by RBA target zone 4.4–4.6 % this year, but shocks (e.g., large participation jump, lay-offs) could briefly push to 4.7–4.8 %.
