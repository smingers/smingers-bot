## (b) Evidence Analysis

### Strong Evidence:

1. **2024 baseline of 65** - Multiple independent sources (Fortune, Mexico News Daily, direct TI data). This is our anchor point. The 2024 score represented a -4 point drop from 2023's 69, the largest single-year decline in the modern series.

2. **FCPA enforcement pause (Jan-Jul 2025)** - Documented in DOJ statements, legal analyses, and news reports. This directly affects CPI source surveys (especially World Economic Forum Executive Opinion Survey and political risk assessments). The pause ended in July 2025, but perception surveys conducted mid-2025 would capture the negative signal.

3. **Historical U.S. volatility pattern** - From 2012-2024: mean annual change -0.75, std dev 1.9. Only one instance of >3-point change (the 2024 drop itself). This suggests mean reversion is typical.

### Moderate Evidence:

4. **Resumed enforcement Q4 2025** - DOJ resumed high-profile cases after July 2025 pause ended. Formation of international anti-corruption task force (US-UK-FR-CH) signals renewed commitment. However, CPI source surveys have varying data collection windows; some may not fully capture this reversal.

5. **Congressional ethics stagnation** - No major ethics reforms passed in 2025. Stock trading ban discussions continued without legislation. Ongoing controversies (per general news context) but no dramatic new scandals at Cabinet level comparable to previous years.

6. **Global trend context** - 148 of 180 countries stagnant or declining since 2012. Western democracies showing slight erosion. U.S. fits broader pattern rather than being an outlier.

### Weak Evidence:

7. **Anecdotal perception** - Various opinion pieces suggest declining trust, but without systematic polling data specific to corruption perceptions.

8. **Timing of survey collection** - CPI aggregates sources with different collection periods. Some use 2024 data, others early 2025. The exact weighting is opaque, making precise prediction difficult.

1. **FCPA pause impact** (moderate-strong): The 6-month enforcement pause was widely publicized and criticized. WEF Executive Opinion Survey (one of 13 CPI sources) likely captured expert views during this period. This is a direct institutional signal of weakened anti-corruption commitment.

2. **Momentum from 2024 drop** (moderate): The –4 point drop in 2024 reflected accumulated concerns. Some of those factors (political polarization, ethics concerns) persisted through 2025.

3. **No major reforms** (weak-moderate): Absence of positive signals. No landmark anti-corruption legislation, no major prosecutions of high-level officials.

**Upward pressures on 2025 score:**
1. **Mean reversion** (strong): Historical pattern shows U.S. rarely sustains large multi-year declines. After the 2024 drop, some rebound is statistically likely.

2. **Enforcement resumption** (moderate): Post-July 2025 DOJ activity and international cooperation may have been captured by surveys conducted in Q3-Q4 2025.

3. **Relative global stability** (weak): No major U.S. corruption scandal in 2025 comparable to other countries' crises (Philippines flood control, Brazil's ongoing issues).

### Net Assessment:
The FCPA pause is the most concrete negative factor, but its impact is partially offset by:
- The pause's limited duration (ended July 2025)
- Historical mean reversion tendency
- Lack of new major scandals
