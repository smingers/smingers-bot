## Outside View Calibration:

**Anchoring on Kyodo Projection:**
Kyodo projects LDP-Ishin coalition to "exceed 233 seats." This is not a probabilistic forecast but a point estimate from current polling. Historical accuracy of Kyodo's first-wave projections (7-10 days out) is generally good but not perfect - they typically capture the winner but can be off by 10-20 seats in close races.

**Translating "exceed 233" to probability:**
- If Kyodo projects 235-240 seats (barely exceeding), with ±15 seat margin of error, probability of falling below 233 would be ~30-40%
- If Kyodo projects 245-250 seats (comfortably exceeding), probability of falling below 233 would be ~15-20%
- The language "exceed" (not "narrowly exceed" or "comfortably exceed") suggests modest confidence

**Base Rate Adjustments:**
- Snap election success rate (100%) is too optimistic given scandal context
- Recent scandal elections (33% success) may be too pessimistic given Takaichi's approval
- Weighted average leaning toward Kyodo's quantitative projection: ~65-75% range

**Uncertainty Factors:**
- 20-30% undecided voters: Could swing ±10-20 seats in either direction
- Untested LDP-Ishin coordination: Adds 5-10% uncertainty
- CRA (CDP-Komeito) coordination: Could be better or worse than expected

**Comparable Probability Scenarios:**
- 90% = 9:1 odds: Would require near-certainty; inappropriate given undecided voters and recent losses
- 75% = 3:1 odds: Reflects Kyodo projection as likely correct but acknowledges meaningful uncertainty
- 65% = 1.86:1 odds: Reflects Kyodo projection with higher weight on recent defeat pattern
- 50% = 1:1 odds: Coin flip; would ignore Kyodo's quantitative data

**Calibration Decision:**
The Kyodo projection is the strongest piece of evidence, based on 162,000 voter sample showing LDP-Ishin leading in enough districts to exceed 233. However:
1. 20-30% undecided introduces real volatility
2. Loss of Komeito organizational support is a significant structural change
3. Recent electoral pattern shows sustained LDP weakness
4. Takaichi's personal popularity is a new positive variable but untested in actual election

Balancing these factors, the probability should be above 50% (respecting Kyodo's data) but well below 90% (acknowledging substantial uncertainty). The 65-72% range seems most appropriate.
