1. **Nepali Communist Party Formation:** The merger of Maoist Centre, Unified Socialist, and eight smaller left parties creates a unified competitor that should perform better than fragmented 2022 left parties. However, Agent report notes critics question grassroots support of some constituent parties. Historical precedent: 2017 UML-Maoist alliance was highly successful, but 2022 fragmentation hurt both. 2. **Gen Z Protest Impact:** 72+ deaths and delegitimization of traditional parties should benefit anti-establishment RSP. The 2023 by-elections showed RSP can win landslides in urban areas. With 52% of voters aged 18-40 and high youth engagement, RSP should significantly outperform 2022. However, RSP's 12.2% 2022 vote share came without running many FPTP candidates; scaling nationally faces organizational challenges.

3. **NC-UML Competition:** Both parties claim to contest independently (unlike National Assembly). Their 2022 vote shares were nearly identical (29.3% vs 30.7%), and both have strong rural networks. Post-protest, both attempted youth rebranding (NC elected 49-year-old Gagan Thapa as leader). Without alliance, competition between them should be fierce, with advantage to whoever better capitalizes on FPTP dynamics.

4. **Electoral System Mechanics:** The 165 FPTP seats create winner-take-all dynamics favoring parties with broad geographic reach and strong local candidates. NC and UML have this infrastructure; RSP and new NCP less so. This systematically advantages traditional parties in seat conversion.

5. **Fragmentation:** 120 parties contesting, including 25% formed post-protests, will split votes and create opportunities for traditional parties with disciplined vote banks to win FPTP seats on pluralities.

**Most Likely Outcome:** Despite protest damage, NC and UML's organizational advantages in FPTP tier should allow one of them to win most seats. NC's slight organizational edge (won National Assembly plurality) and youth rebranding gives marginal advantage. RSP likely improves significantly but organizational limits prevent seat plurality. NCP's performance depends on whether merger translates to ground coordination - early signs (0/18 National Assembly seats) are negative.
