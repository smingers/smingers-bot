2. Reference-class analysis  
Candidate reference classes:  
A. All films with ≥10 Oscar nominations (n≈46 since 1944).  
B. Modern preferential-ballot era (2009-2025) films with ≥10 nominations (n=18).  
C. Extreme-nomination films (≥14 nominations: All About Eve, Titanic, La La Land) (n=3).

Class A is large but combines very different Academy eras.  Class C is too small.  Class B gives a mid-sized sample whose voting rules, branch make-up and category list closely match 2026.  I therefore use Class B but keep an eye on Class C as a sanity check.

4. Justification for outside-view prediction  
• Sinners’ 16 nominations are unprecedented, but two are in brand-new categories (Casting, single Sound branch) and one is Original Song, which are rarely part of multi-win sweeps.  So inflation ≈ +2–3 noms versus the former 14-nom ceiling.  
• In the 2009-25 data, each extra nomination above 10 adds ≈0.4 wins on average.  Extrapolating: expected wins ≈4.8 + (16-10)×0.4 ≈ 7.2, but downward-adjusting 1.5 wins for the “new/weak” categories gives a central estimate ≈5.7.  
• Upside is capped by the historical record (11).  Downside tail (0–1 wins) still exists: American Hustle, The Irishman and True Grit all left empty-handed with 10 noms.  Assign small but non-negligible weight (≈6 %) to 0–1 wins.
