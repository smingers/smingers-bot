4.  How the mixed electoral system allocates seats  
(as explained by the UK Electoral Reform Society article of 1 Apr 2022 and Volt Magyarország primer):

• Total seats: 199.  
• Single-member constituencies (SMCs): 106, elected by first-past-the-post in one round.  
• National list seats: 93, distributed proportionally (d’Hondt) using (a) each party’s separate list vote plus (b) “fragment votes” – votes cast for losing SMC candidates and excess votes above the winner’s total.  
• Thresholds: 5 % for single parties, 10 % for a two-party list, 15 % for lists of three or more. Recognised ethnic-minority lists can win one spokes-seat on about 0.27 %.  
• Biases: because list seats are not fully compensatory and SMC boundaries were redrawn by Fidesz in 2011, the system structurally favours the largest party.  In 2018 Fidesz turned 49 % of votes into 67 % of seats; in 2022, 53 % of votes yielded 68 % of seats (ERS; Robert-Schuman Centre).  Model exercises by Hungarian pollsters show that an opposition force probably needs a national vote lead of 4-5 percentage points to win a simple majority of seats, and an 8-9-point lead for the two-thirds super-majority.
