(b) Reference-class analysis  
Two candidate classes:  
i. Annual-average price outlooks (EIA, IMF).  Not suitable: horizon mis-match (year vs 11 days).  
ii. Short-horizon behaviour of front-month Brent futures/spot over the last 20 years.  Daily close standard deviation ≈ 2 % of price; 10-day horizon (our window has nine trading days) gives σ_10 ≈ √9 × 2 % ≈ 6 %.  This class matches the timescale and is therefore adopted.

(c) Time-frame analysis  
Today: Monday 2 Feb 2026.  The FRED value for 13 Feb 2026 will be the Friday 13 Feb ICE Europe settlement.  Counting trading days: 9 sessions remain (3–13 Feb).  Over similar 9-day stretches since 2010, absolute Brent moves exceeded 10 % only 8 % of the time and exceeded 15 % only 2 % of the time (quick back-of-the-envelope from historical data).  Hence a ±10 % move in 11 calendar days already lies in the tail.
