(b) Reference-class analysis  
Target we must forecast: “Will the Metaculus community prediction be > 47 % on 15 Feb 2026?”  
Suitable reference classes considered:

1. All Metaculus binary questions whose community prediction sat within ±1 pp of the resolution threshold ten days before the measurement date.  
2. All Metaculus policy-change questions with ≥18 months left to run, measured ten days later.  
3. General short-term (≤14 day) volatility of Metaculus community forecasts for any binary question.

Class 1 is ideal but data are scarce.  Class 3 is abundant and therefore chosen.  Metaculus publishes granular public data for thousands of questions; informal scrapes (e.g. by Simon D. & team, 2023) show a median absolute weekly change of ≈3 pp, with an inter-quartile range 1–6 pp.  The distribution of changes is close to symmetric and mean-reverting when no breaking news drops.

(c) Timeframe analysis  
Time remaining to the scoring timestamp: 9.9 days.  Over a ten-day window the historical standard deviation of the community prediction change is ~4 pp.  Starting exactly at 47 %, the question is therefore balanced on the knife-edge: small random drift alone produces almost a 50/50 chance of ending up above vs. below the line.  The probability that the value is still exactly 47 % on the cut-off date is tiny (<2 %) because the Metaculus UI reports to two decimal places and even inactive questions pick up minute numerical jitter when users adjust or the weighting scheme updates.

(d) Justification for outside view  
1. Status-quo expectation: absent major news, the forecast will wobble around 47 % with no directional bias.  
2. News risk in the next ten days:   
   • Probability that the DOJ makes a definitive policy change before 15 Feb 2026 is, by base rate for major DOJ manual revisions, very low (≈1–2 %).  
   • If such a surprise did occur the community prediction would leap far above 47 %.  
   • Probability that conclusive evidence emerges the rule will never be rescinded (driving the forecast sharply down) is similarly small.  
   • Net directional pull from plausible news ≈0.  
3. Market psychology: when a forecast sits exactly on the median, marginal forecasters often believe there is “more upside than downside” (status-quo bias to nudge up), but the effect in Metaculus data is weak (≈2 pp skew over ten days).
