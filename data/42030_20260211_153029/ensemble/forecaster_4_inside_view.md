(d) Justification / inside-view adjustment  
Outside-view base rate (1982-2022) for any Feb-Apr new record year = 44 %.  
Step-change warmth in 2023-25 pushed daily records up another ~0.3 °C.  January observations demonstrate 2026 now sits ~0.25 °C below those fresh records.  Daily intra-seasonal SD of basin-mean SST ≈0.10 °C; beating the 2025 curve therefore requires a ≥2.5 σ positive swing on at least one day.  Autocorrelation reduces independent trials to ≈8–10 “weekly” attempts.  Assuming Gaussian anomalies with mean −0.25 and σ 0.10 °C, single-trial exceedance ≈1 %.  Weekly-block chance ≈1 – (0.99)^9 ≈ 9 %.  

That purely statistical figure is raised for two reasons:  
• Structural warming/aerosol forcing (+8  p.p.).  
• Observed post-2022 incidence of multi-day basin-scale heatwaves suggests tail-risk is fatter than Gaussian (+6  p.p.).  

Conversely, persistence of La Niña through March subtracts 5 p.p.  

Resulting inside-view probability ≈ 18–20 %.  I add a 5 p.p. cushion for deep uncertainty (model error, potential extreme event), landing at 23 %.
