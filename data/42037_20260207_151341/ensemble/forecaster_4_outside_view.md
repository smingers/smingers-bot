(b) Reference class analysis  

Candidate classes:  
i. Ten previous February values (2016-25) of the exact EIA series.  
ii. The last six monthly observations (Aug 25 – Jan 26).  
iii. One-month-ahead errors in EIA STEO gasoline forecasts (historic).  

Class (i) is long-run but absorbs structural regime shifts (e.g., COVID, Ukraine war) and gives a wide distribution (μ = 2.71, σ = 0.54).  Class (ii) focuses on the immediate price level regime (μ = 3.16, σ = 0.13).  Class (iii) would target forecast error rather than price itself; historic 1-σ error ≈ 0.15 $/gal.  

Because we are forecasting one month out and know January’s actual, class (ii) supplemented by historic one-month forecast error (≈ 0.15) is the most informative and avoids over-weighting ancient, low-price years.  I adopt class (ii) as baseline, then widen the uncertainty by the historic 0.15 $/gal error band.
