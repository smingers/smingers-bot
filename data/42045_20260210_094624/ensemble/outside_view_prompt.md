**Source:** Contagion Live

## Key Facts and Statistics:

### Malaria-Free Certifications:
- **WHO 2025 World Malaria Report recognizes 47 countries and one territory as malaria free**
- **Most recently certified countries: Egypt, Georgia, Suriname, and Timor-Leste**

### Regional Progress and Challenges:

**Greater Mekong Subregion (GMS):**
- Cambodia, Lao People's Democratic Republic, and Vietnam are nearing elimination of indigenous cases
- These countries were previously "the epicenter of resistance" but achieved near-elimination through coordinated efforts

**WHO Africa Region:**
- Accounts for 94% of malaria cases and 95% of deaths globally
- Five countries account for more than half of all global cases in 2024: Democratic Republic of the Congo, Ethiopia, Mozambique, Nigeria, and Uganda
- Between 2015 and 2024: 2% reduction in case incidence and 17% decline in mortality rate
- 2024 malaria incidence: 64 cases per 1,000 population at risk (more than 3 times the target of 18 cases per 1,000)

### Global Technical Strategy (GTS) Targets:
- Target reductions in case incidence and mortality rates: at least 75% by 2025 and 90% by 2030 (compared to 2015 baseline)
- Africa Region's progress is more than double the target levels (meaning they are falling short)

### Drug and Insecticide Resistance:
- P. falciparum Kelch 13 (Pf Kelch 13) gene mutations associated with artemisinin partial resistance are emerging from multiple independent origins in Africa
- Anopheles stephensi (invasive mosquito species resistant to commonly used insecticides) detected in 9 African countries
- This mosquito species thrives in urban settings and high temperatures associated with climate change

### P. knowlesi Parasite:
- Over 2,000 reported cases in 2024 (34% decrease from 2023)
- 1-2% human fatality rate
- Cases predominantly in Malaysia (89%), Indonesia (6%), Thailand (4%), and Cambodia (0.5%)

## Opinions and Attributions:

**WHO (via the 2025 World Malaria Report):**
- Credits "strong national leadership, targeted use of effective treatments, robust surveillance and sustained community engagement" for substantial reduction in GMS cases
- Warns that repeated emergence and spread of artemisinin partial resistance presents challenges in a context where treatment depends on limited combinations
- Encourages broad public health efforts beyond core malaria interventions, including improved housing, nutrition, education, and access to primary care
