# Summary of Article: "Third Draft of the General-Purpose AI Code of Practice published, written by independent experts"

## Key Facts and Timeline:

1. **Third draft publication**: The European Commission's AI Office has published a third draft of the General-Purpose AI (GPAI) Code of Practice.

2. **Finalization timeline**: This is described as "the last drafting round" with the final Code expected to be ready in **May [2025]**, based on stakeholder feedback to this proposal.

3. **Previous drafts**: This follows a second draft published on **December 19, 2024**.

## Structure and Content of Third Draft:

**For all GPAI model providers:**
- 2 Commitments related to transparency and copyright
- Includes a user-friendly Model Documentation Form for transparency documentation
- Notable exemptions from transparency obligations for certain open-source models

**For GPAI models with systemic risk (Article 51 of AI Act):**
- 16 additional Commitments related to safety and security
- Measures include systemic risk assessment and mitigation, model evaluations, incident reporting, and cybersecurity obligations

**Improvements:**
- More streamlined structure with refined commitments and measures
- Based on feedback from the second draft
- Includes a dedicated executive summary and interactive website to facilitate stakeholder feedback

## Stakeholder Process:

- Approximately **1000 stakeholders** participating in Code of Practice Working Groups and Provider Workshops
- Includes EU Member State representatives and European/international observers
- Written feedback deadline: **Sunday, March 30, 2025**
- Additional workshops planned for providers, Member States, civil society organizations, and downstream industry

## Complementary AI Office Actions:

The AI Office will separately publish guidance on:
- Definitions of GPAI models
- Placing of models on the market and provider responsibilities
- Value chain responsibilities (downstream actors modifying/fine-tuning models)
- Exemptions for free and open-source licensed models
- Effects on models placed on market before August 2025
