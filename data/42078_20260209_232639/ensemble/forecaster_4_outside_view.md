B. Reference-class analysis  
Candidate classes:
1. Iran-only nationwide shutdown episodes (2019 fuel-price protests, 2022 “Women, Life, Freedom”). Very close match institutionally and technologically (same authorities, same National Information Network). Limitation: N = 2.  
2. Authoritarian protest-driven shutdowns of ≥5 days globally since 2015 (Iran 2019, Iran 2022, Myanmar 2021, Ethiopia-Tigray 2020-22, Kazakhstan 2022, Sri Lanka 2022, Uganda 2021, etc.). Broader N (~10) but mixes cases where whitelisting/segmentation became semi-permanent (Myanmar, Ethiopia).  
3. Long-term “whitelist architecture” roll-outs (Kashmir 2019–2020, Myanmar post-coup). Captures the special-case technology but includes only a handful of observations.

Suitability: Class 2 balances relevance and sample size: all are political shutdowns that resulted in some form of “heavy filtering/whitelist”, and the main outcome of interest is whether most users regained comparatively open connectivity within three months.

Empirical base rate from class 2 (compiled from NetBlocks & Access Now reports):  
– Resolved to pre-crackdown level within 3 months: Iran 2019, Kazakhstan 2022, Sri Lanka 2022, Uganda 2021, Iran 2022, Sudan 2019 → 6 / 10 ≈ 60 %  
– Still “heavily filtered/whitelisted” after 3 months: Myanmar 2021, Ethiopia-Tigray 2020, Kashmir (India-administered) 2019, Belarus partial 2020 → 4 / 10 ≈ 40 %

D. Justification for outside-view probability  
1. Structural similarity: Iran has twice before moved from severe disruption to its traditional (lighter) filtering in ≤3 months despite political unrest.  
2. Economic cost: historical data (2019, 2022) show authorities bowing to business-sector pressure; comparable patterns existed in 60 % of global cases that reverted.  
3. Counter-examples exist where regimes accepted prolonged isolation (Myanmar, Ethiopia). These tended to coincide with either civil war or military rule—conditions more extreme than Iran’s current intra-elite crisis, but still cautionary.  
4. Expert signals in the present cycle (official “maybe by Nowruz”) are excluded from the outside view by definition, but historically, public official timelines have been roughly right half the time; neutral for the base rate.
