*Upward pressures:*
1. **Momentum from 2025:** The strong upward trend (27→33→45) suggests underlying drivers remain
2. **Economic activity:** Global trade volumes typically drive opportunity for maritime crime
3. **Pirate capability:** Gulf of Guinea groups remain capable; November 2025 Somalia incidents showed long-range capability persists
4. **Regression potential:** If enforcement effects fade, Singapore Straits could see resurgence

**Central Estimate:**
The enforcement crackdown in Singapore Straits is the most significant new factor. Given that region contributed ~26 incidents to Q1 2025 (58% of 45), a 30-40% reduction there would suggest 8-10 fewer incidents, pointing to 35-37 incidents for Q1 2026. However, other regions may partially offset this.

Balancing the strong 2023-2025 upward trend against the specific enforcement intervention, I estimate Q1 2026 will likely fall between 30-42 incidents, with a central tendency around 36-38.
