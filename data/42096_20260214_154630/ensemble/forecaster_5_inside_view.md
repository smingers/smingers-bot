(b) Evidence weighing  
Strong:  
• IMB 2025 data showing half-year collapse of Singapore-Straits incidents after July arrests (structural enforcement shift).  
Moderate:  
• ReCAAP and IMB both observe continued enforcement patrols into 2026.  
• Gulf of Guinea kidnappings ticked up to 23 crew in 2025 (could raise 2026 incidents slightly).  
Weak:  
• Individual late-2025 hijack/kidnap news items; anecdotal.  
• Media opinion pieces about “surge of piracy” lacking numbers.

(c) Timeframe analysis  
Predicted window: 1 Jan – 31 Mar 2026 (46 days remain until period end; ≈ 72 days to IMB release).  
If the window were halved (three weeks left), uncertainty would drop only a little because most incidents occur throughout the quarter. If doubled (extend to H1), variance would widen markedly as seasonal and enforcement effects compound.

(d) Justification of inside-view shift  
Baseline (outside view) median 48 derives from eight-year Q1 mean (41) plus partial trend extrapolation.  
Key current factors:  
1. Singapore Straits—58 % of 2025 global incidents—experienced a documented slump in H2-2025 after two gangs were dismantled. Regional navies have kept extra patrols through Jan-Feb 2026 (confirmed by Singapore MPA circular 01/26). That removes ~15 “expected” events for Q1. (Strong evidence, direct causal).  
2. Gulf of Guinea incidents rose modestly (21 in 2025) and early-Jan 2026 saw one boarding with 6 kidnapped (Dryad report, 08-Jan-2026). Adds perhaps +3 over Q1-2025. (Moderate evidence).  
3. No material resurgence reported off Somalia; Navy presence stable. (Moderate).  
Net effect: −12 relative to last year’s Q1 (45 → ≈ 33). Given model and reporting noise, I push the median down from 48 to 38 and narrow the right tail slightly; left tail kept wide because a sudden collapse in petty theft reporting is plausible.
