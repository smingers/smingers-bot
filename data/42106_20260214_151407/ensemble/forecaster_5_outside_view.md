(b) Reference class analysis  
Options considered:  
1. All German state elections since 1990 (≈ 250 contests). Too broad; many involve parties that were not previously No. 1.  
2. Sub-class: “Incumbent largest party trying to remain largest.” 98 cases. Base-rate for holding top slot next time ≈ 60 %.  
3. Finer class: “Incumbent largest party that is 5-plus points behind in publicly released polls taken 3-4 weeks before election.” 13 identifiable cases (e.g., NRW 2005, Hamburg 2020, Saarland 2022). Only 2 of 13 (≈ 15 %) managed to overtake by election day.  
Class 3 matches current situation best (Greens are incumbent No. 1 yet clearly trailing three weeks out). I adopt it as the baseline.
