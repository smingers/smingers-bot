(d) Justification  
Outside view deliberately ignores current-year idiosyncrasies (January’s 19 units, the new Tianjin & Toulouse lines not yet ramped, any fresh parts shortages). Instead it treats 2026 as another draw from the historical March distribution:

• Centre of the distribution: ≈ 60.  
• Down-side tail: Covid-type interruptions (2020) showed March could fall to the mid-30s. A comparable one-month supply shock has ~10 % historical frequency.  
• Up-side tail: Best March so far is 72 (2021). Hitting the low-70s required both improved supply chain and pent-up demand release; that remains plausible at the 10-20 % level.  
• Extreme outcomes (<30 or >80) have no precedent in the 2018-25 set, so they stay in low single-digit territory.

Outside view calibration:

1. Anchor the mean at 60 and σ at 10.  
2. Impose a floor at 0 and recognise fat-tails by widening the 10th/90th percentiles relative to the Gaussian −1.28σ / +1.28σ positions.  
3. Historical “upset” rate (outcome outside ±1.5 σ) is 1/8 ≈ 12 % (2020). That justifies a somewhat broader lower tail.  
4. Upper tail is capped by production reality: Airbus has never exceeded 75 deliveries before April of any year; the 90th percentile therefore sits below 80.
