(b) Reference class analysis  
Candidates are competing in a statewide, non-partisan, spring (April) Wisconsin Supreme Court general election with only one seat on the ballot.  Plausible reference classes:  
• All Wisconsin Supreme Court general elections since the non-partisan system began (19 contests, 1990-2025).  Advantage: large N, but includes many incumbent-protection races.  
• Contests in which the seat was open (no incumbent) – nine races 1995-2025.  Better fit here.  
• Open-seat spring races in which the outcome would NOT flip majority control (1995, 2009, 2019, 2026).  Best thematic match but N=3, too small.  
Chosen class: the nine open-seat general elections, 1995-2025.  They capture voter behaviour when neither side enjoys an incumbent advantage and avoid the “safe” re-elections that inflate conservative margins earlier in the period.
