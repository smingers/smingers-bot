4. **Historical Context:**
   - Washington was born in Westmoreland County, Virginia
   - He became commander-in-chief of revolutionary America's armed forces
   - He was elected president in 1789 and reelected in 1792

   - On February 10, 1782, the comte de Rochambeau wrote to Washington announcing plans to celebrate Washington's birthday with a ball on February 11, 1782

# Summary of Article: "George Washington's Birthday aka Presidents Day"

## Key Facts and Information:

**Federal vs. State Recognition:**
- The U.S. federal government still officially recognizes the holiday as "George Washington's Birthday," not "Presidents Day"
- Washington's birthday (February 22nd) was proclaimed a federal holiday in 1879
- The Uniform Monday Holiday Act in 1971 moved the observance to the third Monday of February in Oregon and other states

**Varying State Observances:**
- The holiday has 15 separate names across the U.S.
- Virginia, Illinois, Iowa, and New York specifically recognize it as "Washington's Birthday" or "George Washington Day"
- Virginia celebrates Washington's birthday for the entire month
- Laredo, Texas has held an annual Washington Birthday Celebration lasting the entire month since 1898

**Federal Tradition:**
- Since 1896, a current U.S. Senate member reads "Washington's Farewell Address" on February 22nd as a tradition

**Modern vs. Historical Observance:**
- Today's Presidents Day celebrates both Washington and Abraham Lincoln (born February 12) in a relatively low-key manner, primarily marked by sales and government building closures
- Historical celebrations were much more elaborate, as evidenced by 1892 Medford, Oregon school exercises featuring 76 recitations, songs, and readings lasting over two hours

- The article includes anecdotal information about early 20th century Lincoln Day Banquet controversies in Medford, Oregon (1915) and the death of prominent Jacksonville citizen Cornelius C. Beekman on February 22, 1915
