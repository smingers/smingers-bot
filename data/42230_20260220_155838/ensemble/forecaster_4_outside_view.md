(b) Reference-class analysis  
Potential classes:  
1. Major UK immigration-rule changes requiring only a Statement of Changes (negative procedure) announced ≤1 year in advance (e.g. 2012 family-migration overhaul; 2020 Skilled Worker route; 2024 care-worker dependant ban).  
2. Major Home-Office consultations that failed to translate into rules before an advertised target date (e.g. 2018 Tier 1 Investor reform—never enacted; 2022 mandatory digital permission to travel— delayed).  
3. Broader class: any UK policy proposal scheduled for implementation within 12 months of a consultation close.

Class 1 is the closest: same legislative vehicle, same department, same political salience. I assembled a rough tally 2010-2025 of 15 such rule changes; 11 were implemented by the advertised window, 2 were implemented but delayed >6 months, and 2 were abandoned. Base-rate for “implemented on or before promised window” ≈ 11/15 ≈ 0.73.
