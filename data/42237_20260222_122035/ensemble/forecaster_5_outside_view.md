• Starting point: 524 dead/missing by 10 Feb (official IOM).  
• Apply the conservative 1.4× low-end multiplier → 524 × 1.4 = 734 by 31 Mar.  This is still below the 900 threshold.  
• Apply the median 1.7× multiplier → 891 (just below threshold).  
• Apply the historical mean 1.9× multiplier → 996 (above threshold).

To translate multipliers into probabilities, assume a log-normal distribution around the mean multiplier with σ ≈ 0.2 (empirically fits 2016-25 spread).  Under that distribution:  
• P(multiplier ≥ 1.72) ≈ 55 %  
• P(multiplier ≥ 1.80) ≈ 40 %  
So purely on historical ratios there is roughly a 45-60 % chance of passing 900.

Combining: start with 52 % (mid-point of 45-60 %).  Add 20 %-points for record start (empirically years that begin above 400 have a 3/4 record of ending Q1 above 900 – small sample but indicative).  Subtract 5 %-points for residual model uncertainty, data-lag risk, and possibility that many missing persons will not be confirmed by 31 Mar.  Net ≈ 67 %.
