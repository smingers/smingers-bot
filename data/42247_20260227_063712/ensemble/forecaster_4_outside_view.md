7-9. Round-3 recap pieces (PlanetRugby, CorinthianSports, RugbyIsTheGame). Provide factual match scores and table context; also contain subjective judgements (“England in tatters”, “France favourites”). As opinion they are not from recognised forecasters; I treat them as colour only.  
10. Agent-report about missing round-3-leader dataset. Factual about data availability; no new substantive rugby information.

Overall, only sources 1–4 materially inform an historical (outside-view) baseline; the R3 recaps drift into an “inside view” and are therefore deliberately down-weighted for this exercise.

(b)  Reference class analysis  
Candidate classes:  
• RC-1: All Six Nations tournaments 2000-2025 (26 seasons).  
• RC-2: The last 10 seasons (2016-2025) – reflects current professional era, coaching, athlete pathways.  
• RC-3: Whole Home/Five/Six Nations history since 1883 – too broad; competitive balance and Italy’s presence differ.  

RC-2 is chosen: it is recent enough to match today’s squads, laws and bonus-point era (since 2017) while still giving 10 data points – a compromise between recency and sample size.

Title frequencies in RC-2:  
Ireland 3, France 2, England 2, Wales 2, Scotland 0, Italy 0.  Normalised to 100 % → IRE 30 %, FRA 20 %, ENG 20 %, WAL 20 %, SCO 0 %, ITA 0 %.

(c)  Time-frame analysis  
Days until the 2026 champion is known: 16 days to the final match (14 Mar) and at most ~60 days to formal resolution.  Historically, about 70 % of seasons have seen the R3 table-leader go on to win (informal ESPN/Scrum audit of 2017-2025).  However, using that information would step toward an inside view; for an outside baseline I observe only that three rounds remain – meaning late reversals are not uncommon (~30 % of seasons).

(d)  Justification  
1.  RC-2 tells us the tournament has been essentially a four-horse race (IRE, FRA, ENG, WAL).  
2.  Scotland have improved but have never converted promise into silverware; Italy have never been within one win of the title.  Their outside-view chances should be low but non-zero.  
3.  A small recent trend advantage exists for Ireland and France (five of the last six titles), so I tilt some mass from England/Wales toward those two.  
4.  Upset rate: 0 titles out of 26 for either Scotland or Italy → empirical prior < 4 %.  I assign 2 % Italy, 8 % Scotland (slightly higher for Scotland owing to competitive performances in recent seasons).  
5.  Remaining 90 % is split among the traditional four, weighted by RC-2 counts but nudged toward current era dominance (Ireland +2 pp, France +8 pp, England –2 pp, Wales –8 pp).

Outside view calibration:

• Anchor: RC-2 frequencies.  
• Adjust for zero-title nations but acknowledge small upset probability → give Scotland and Italy 10 % combined.  
• Re-allocate the deducted 10 % mostly to France (recent champion) and slightly to Ireland.  
Final sanity check: no team above one-third probability (reflects historic variability); long-shots receive low single digits.
