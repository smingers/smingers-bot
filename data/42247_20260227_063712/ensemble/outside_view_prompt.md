## Summary: Six Nations Points System Explained (BBC Sport, January 30, 2025)

### Competition Format
- Round-robin format: 6 teams, each playing 5 matches
- Home advantage alternates each season

### Points System
| Result | Points |
|--------|--------|
| Win | 4 points |
| Draw | 2 points |
| Loss | 0 points |

### Bonus Points (introduced in 2017)
- **+1 point** for scoring 4+ tries in a single match
- **+1 point** for losing by 7 or fewer points
- **+3 points** for winning all five matches (Grand Slam bonus)
- Maximum points per game: **5** (win + try bonus)

### Key Structural Notes
- The Grand Slam bonus (3 pts) is designed to **guarantee the Grand Slam winner tops the table** — a Grand Slam winner earns at least 23 points, which cannot be surpassed by a team winning only 4 games
- **Tiebreakers** (in order): points difference → total tries scored

### Notable Historical Examples Referenced
- **2020**: England won title on points difference over France
- **2022**: England finished above Scotland (both 10 pts) on points difference (+5 vs -29)
- **2024**: England's two bonus points in a loss to France proved decisive over Scotland in final standings
