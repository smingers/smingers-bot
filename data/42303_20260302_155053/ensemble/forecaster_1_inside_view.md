## (b) Evidence Analysis

**Strong evidence:**
1. **Mediana poll (Feb 16, 2026) — multiple independent confirmations**: SDS projected 30 seats, GS projected 23 seats → difference of **+7**. This is the most recent seat-level projection from a reputable polling firm, confirmed by multiple independent Austrian outlets.

2. **Consistent polling lead across multiple surveys**: Three independent polls (Ninamedia Jan 26-27, Siol Jan 28, Mediana Feb 16) all show SDS leading GS by 3.4-4.2 percentage points in vote share. This consistency across different polling firms and methodologies is strong evidence of a genuine SDS lead.

3. **GS incumbent disadvantage**: Historical pattern in Slovenia — governing parties typically lose seats (SD 2008, SMC 2014, LMŠ 2018 all underperformed). GS won 41 seats in 2022 under exceptional anti-Janša mobilization conditions unlikely to repeat.

**Moderate evidence:**
4. **Resni.ca spoiler effect**: Projected at 3.5-5.3% — near the 4% threshold. If it clears, it fragments the right-wing vote but doesn't help GS. If it falls below threshold, its votes redistribute proportionally, potentially benefiting SDS. This creates uncertainty in the +/- 2 seat range.

5. **High undecided voter share (19.7%)**: Could break either way. Historically in Slovenia, undecideds have sometimes broken toward challengers of incumbents. Slight downward pressure on GS's final result.

6. **Janša's acquittal (April 2025) and referendum win (November 2025)**: Both events boosted SDS's narrative and mobilization capacity. Moderate evidence for SDS outperforming polls slightly.

**Weak evidence:**
7. **Janša's controversial rhetoric** ("close the taps"): Could alienate moderate voters, potentially limiting SDS's ceiling. Weak evidence — Janša's rhetoric has historically not significantly hurt him.

8. **Anti-Janša mobilization risk**: In 2022, GS dramatically outperformed (+11 pp). This is the main tail risk. However, GS is now the incumbent, making a repeat much less likely. Weak evidence for GS outperformance.

## (c) Timeframe Analysis

**20 days until election (March 22, 2026)**. This is a very short window. Polls are relatively stable at this stage. The main uncertainties are:
- Last-minute mobilization effects
- Whether smaller parties clear the 4% threshold
- Debate performance (if any major debates occur)

If the timeframe were halved (10 days), I would have more confidence in the current polling picture holding. If doubled (40 days), there would be more opportunity for a GS recovery or SDS surge, widening the distribution.

At 20 days, the distribution should be moderately tight around the polling-implied estimate, with meaningful tails for polling errors.

## (d) Justification

**Central estimate**: The Mediana poll (most recent, seat-level projection) shows SDS +7 seats. The PolitPro aggregator (from outside view analysis) showed SDS +9 seats. These two estimates bracket a reasonable central range of **+7 to +9**.

**Adjustments from base rate**:
- The outside view reference class (historical 1st-2nd party gaps) averages ~+9.6 seats. This is consistent with current polling.
- GS's 2022 result (41 seats) was exceptional and unlikely to repeat. The regression toward the mean suggests GS will win significantly fewer seats.
- The polling consensus is unusually consistent across firms, reducing the uncertainty around the central estimate.
- The 2022 GS surge (+11 pp vs. polls) is the main tail risk pushing toward GS outperformance, but this is less likely given incumbent status.
- Threshold effects from Resni.ca and other small parties add ±2-3 seat uncertainty.

**Key insight**: The question asks for SDS seats MINUS GS seats. The central estimate of +7 to +9 is well-supported. The distribution should be:
- Left tail (GS wins more): Requires a 2022-style mobilization surge, which seems unlikely but not impossible (~10-15% probability of GS winning more seats)
- Right tail (SDS wins by 15+): Requires GS to collapse further and/or threshold effects to amplify SDS's lead
