## (d) Justification

**Starting point**: PolitPro projects SDS 31 seats, GS 22 seats → difference of +9.

**Adjustments**:
1. **GS as incumbent**: Incumbents in Slovenia have faced voter fatigue (2008 SD, 2014 SMC, 2018 LMŠ all underperformed). GS's 41-seat 2022 result was exceptional; regression toward the mean is expected.
2. **Anti-Janša mobilization risk**: In 2022, turnout surged and tactical voting massively benefited GS. This is less likely in 2026 since GS is the incumbent and Janša's acquittal reduces the "threat" narrative. However, some mobilization remains possible.
3. **Undecided voters**: A large undecided share could break either way. Historically in Slovenia, undecideds have sometimes broken toward the challenger of the incumbent.
4. **Threshold effects**: If parties like Resnica (4.3% projected) fall below 4%, their votes are redistributed proportionally, potentially benefiting SDS more.
5. **Polling error**: Given ~3.5 pp error for SDS and ~4.8 pp for GS, the seat difference could vary by ±7-10 seats from the central estimate.

**Central estimate**: SDS wins approximately 8-10 more seats than GS. The PolitPro +9 projection seems reasonable as a central estimate.

**Distribution shape**: The distribution should be centered around +7 to +10, with meaningful probability of outcomes ranging from SDS winning by only 1-2 seats (if GS mobilizes effectively) to SDS winning by 15+ seats (if GS collapses further). The left tail (GS winning more seats than SDS) is possible but requires a repeat of 2022-style mobilization, which seems less likely given GS's incumbent status.
