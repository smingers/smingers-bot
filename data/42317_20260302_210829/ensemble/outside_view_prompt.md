## Summary of Article: PIF ATP Live Rankings FAQ

This article is an FAQ explaining the **PIF ATP Live Rankings** system and how it differs from the traditional **PIF ATP Rankings**.

### Key Distinctions Between the Two Systems

- **PIF ATP Rankings** (traditional/official): Used for tournament entry, seedings, and milestone achievements (e.g., weeks at No. 1, Top 10 status). Updated weekly on Sunday nights. Has been in operation since **August 23, 1973**.
- **PIF ATP Live Rankings**: A real-time, dynamic ranking calculated as matches finish during a tournament week. Used for narrative/fan engagement purposes only — **not** for milestones or official standings.

### How Live Rankings Work
- Points are added and dropped dynamically as matches conclude.
- The Live Rankings **reset on Sunday nights**, removing points earned 52 weeks prior.
- At **mandatory events** (Grand Slams, Masters 1000s), all points earned count immediately toward live totals.
- At **non-mandatory events**, new points only increase a player's total if they exceed the player's lowest non-countable score.

### Important Limitations
- A player **cannot claim World No. 1** status based solely on Live Rankings — only official weekly ATP Rankings confer that title.
- Similarly, temporary Top 10 appearances in Live Rankings do **not** make a player a "Top 10 player."
