**Scale & Progress:**
- Over 800,000 votes collected
- More than 90 LLMs evaluated
- Millions of participants engaged
- Models evaluated include commercial models (GPT-4, Gemini/Bard) and open-weight models (Llama, Mistral)

- **Open source:** Full platform (FastChat) is publicly available on GitHub

- **Leaderboard eligibility:** Only models accessible to third parties (open weights, public APIs, or available as a service) appear on the public leaderboard

- **Unreleased models:** Can be tested anonymously in preview mode before public release
- **Data sharing:** 20% of vote data (including prompts, answers, model identities, and votes) is periodically shared with the community

**Funding:** Funded solely by gifts (monetary, cloud credits, API credits) with no strings attached.

- Known biases include: **human preference for longer responses and formatting**, **confirmation bias**, and **non-reproducibility** (noted as a criticism by Clémentine Fourrier of Hugging Face).
- The team uses **logistic regression ("style control")** to decompose human preferences into constituent factors and mathematically isolate core model capabilities from stylistic influences.
- This approach allows post-hoc creation of adjusted leaderboards (e.g., cost-adjusted, parameter-adjusted).

- Labs could theoretically test multiple private variants and only submit the best performer.
