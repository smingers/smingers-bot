### (b) Reference class analysis (candidate baselines)

Because the policy environment since 2025 appears to have created a **structural break** to much lower encounter volumes, the best outside-view reference class should overweight **recent low-regime months** rather than 2010–2023.

Candidate reference classes:
1) **“Low-regime” months under the current enforcement posture (roughly Feb 2025–Jan 2026)**  
   - Pros: Most similar operational/policy regime; aligns with the dramatic shift documented by Pew and CBP releases.  
   - Cons: Short history; uncertainty about durability and seasonality.

2) **Trump first-term low months (e.g., 2017 spring lows ~11k–15k in some sources for USBP-only)**  
   - Pros: Same broad political party/policy direction; gives a sense of plausible “floor” absent the newer tools/conditions.  
   - Cons: Not the same era; and again metric mismatch unless using total encounters.

3) **Long-run March distribution (2010–2024)**
   - Pros: True seasonality reference for March.  
   - Cons: Dominated by years with far higher volumes; likely misleading given the post-2025 level shift.

**Most suitable:** (1) the **recent low-regime** reference class, anchored to official **Jan 2026 SW encounters = 9,726** and the cited **Sep 2025 ≈ 11,647**, plus “historic low” months around spring/summer 2025 (likely single-digit thousands for USBP-only).
