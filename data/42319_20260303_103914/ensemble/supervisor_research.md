## Summary: U.S. Southwest Border Encounters Drop Significantly in January 2024

**Source:** Houston Public Media / Texas Public Radio | **Date:** February 14, 2024 | **Authors:** Marian Navarro & Dan Katz

### Key Statistics
- **U.S. Border Patrol encounters between ports of entry:** 124,220 in January 2024
- **Total encounters** (including ports of entry and CBP One appointments): **176,205**
- This represents a **50% drop** in between-port encounters and a **42% drop** in total encounters compared to December 2023
- Described as the **lowest recorded number in fiscal year 2024** at that point
- Despite the drop, January 2024 encounters were **still higher** than the same month in prior years

### Official Statements
- **Troy Miller, acting CBP Commissioner:** Attributed the decline to seasonal trends and enhanced enforcement by CBP and international partners
- Miller also noted: *"We continue to experience serious challenges along our border which surpass the capacity of the immigration system"*

### Contributing Factors Cited
- Increased U.S.-Mexico coordination, with Mexican officials cracking down on smuggling networks and increasing border patrols
- Clearance of a migrant encampment in Matamoros (across from Brownsville) that previously held ~1,500 people

### Political Context
- The drop coincided with the Republican-led House voting to **impeach DHS Secretary Alejandro Mayorkas**, which DHS called constitutionally unfounded

### Key Statistics
- **January 2026**: Approximately **6,100 detected illegal crossing attempts** (between official ports of entry, USBP apprehensions/expulsions only)
- This represents a **79.1% decrease** compared to January 2025
- The **largest sector decrease** was in **San Diego**, down 87.5% year-over-year (January 2025 to January 2026)
- The **highest activity sector** in January 2026 was the **Rio Grande Valley**

### Important Methodological Notes
- These figures represent **USBP encounters between ports of entry only** — they are **not the same as CBP's total Southwest Land Border Encounters**, which also include Office of Field Operations (OFO) encounters at ports of entry
- One person may be **counted multiple times** if they make multiple crossing attempts
- DHS estimated USBP intercepted ~**80% of attempted crossings** in 2021 (most recent available data)

### Historical Context
- Crossings peaked at **249,700 in December 2023**, then declined throughout 2024
- Title 42 (March 2020–May 2023) contributed to elevated recidivism rates, nearly **quadrupling** pre-pandemic levels

### Key Definitional Information Provided:

- **Encounter data** encompasses three categories:
  - **Title 8 Apprehensions** (U.S. Border Patrol/USBP): Physical control or temporary detainment of persons between ports of entry (POEs) not lawfully in the U.S.
  - **Title 8 Inadmissibles** (Office of Field Operations/OFO): Individuals at POEs deemed inadmissible, including those seeking humanitarian protection.
  - **Title 42 Expulsions**: Individuals expelled under public health authority — applicable only from **March 21, 2020 to May 11, 2023** (no longer active).

- Data is extracted from **live CBP systems** and is subject to change due to corrections, system changes, or pending reviews. **Final statistics are published at the conclusion of each fiscal year.**

- Sector- and field office-level breakdowns are available via a separate "By Component" page, and raw data is accessible through the **CBP Data Portal**.
