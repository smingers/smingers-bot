(d) Justification

Status quo outcome if current trend continued  
The clearest status quo is: 2026 remains another high-flow year, with March-April producing a seasonal upswing, while deterrence measures remain too small or too new to materially reduce totals by Apr 30. That status quo points squarely to 10,001–20,000.

What shifts me from the outside view
- The outside view already favored 10,001–20,000 at 56. The strongest inside-view fact is the observed 2,209 by Feb 25, 7% above same point in 2025.
- Since 2025 appears to have had March-April daily averages around 148, a straightforward continuation lands in the low teens by Apr 30.
- This is direct, current-year evidence, so it warrants increasing the 10,001–20,000 bin and reducing 5,001–10,000 and especially 0–5,000.

Why not shift even more aggressively?
- Weather volatility remains large over a 55-day horizon.
- The 10,001 threshold is not already locked in; it still requires a decent spring pace from here (~122/day from Feb 26 onward).
- New UK restrictions and incremental French action could trim flows at the margin, even if not enough for a structural break.
- Therefore 5,001–10,000 still deserves substantial probability.

Why 20,001+ stays low
- Starting from 2,209 on Feb 25, getting above 20,001 by Apr 30 requires ~278/day for the rest of the period.
- That is nearly double the cited 2025 March-April average and would imply an extraordinary, sustained surge in a still partly weather-constrained season.
- It is possible if there are many calm days and very large boat occupancies, but it is clearly a tail scenario.

Why 0–5,000 becomes very unlikely
- Need only ~44/day average for the rest of the period to exceed 5,000.
- Given observed early-March crossings, ongoing camp population, and recent-year spring seasonality, a sub-5,000 finish would likely require a highly disrupted March-April from storms and/or a sudden enforcement breakthrough. Possible, but now quite unlikely.
