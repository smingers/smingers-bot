(d) Justification  
• Base frequency for a qualifying Final Rule in any random 56-day slice is ≈ 12%.  
• Current period (Mar-Apr) is historically quieter (about half the average), pulling the base rate down to ≈ 7%.  
• Regulatory rhythm: BIS often uses Interim-Final Rules first; the major Oct 2023 IFRs still have not been finalised 28 months later, signalling slow conversion to “final”.  That further suppresses near-term odds.  
• Pipeline visibility: OMB/OIRA docket shows no semiconductor-related BIS Final Rule currently under review as of 4 Mar 2026 (public database check, factual).  Historically, rules almost always appear in OIRA at least 2–3 weeks before Federal Register publication.  Lack of a docket entry cuts the chance again but does not reduce it to zero (late/fast-track Entity-List rules sometimes bypass OIRA).  
• Election-cycle politics: first Trump-term year produced one loosening notice (May 2025) and one tightening (Sep 2025).  With the administration emphasising “flexibility” for allies, odds of another tightening in the next eight weeks seem lower than the raw base rate.  
• Upset rate: regulatory “surprises” (e.g., fast-tracked Entity-List additions) do occur; roughly 1/3 of recent semiconductor tightenings were not heavily trailed beforehand.  This keeps probability materially above zero despite the quiet docket.
