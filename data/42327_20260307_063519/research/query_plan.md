Analysis:
The pre-research context only confirms where IAEA reports live; it does not tell us 1) how often IAEA reports come out in the Feb–Apr window, 2) how often they have contained “installed” cascade language in the past, 3) what the current expansion status at Natanz/Fordow is, or 4) what political/technical factors could accelerate or halt new cascade installation before May 1 2026.  
A skilled forecaster therefore still needs:

Background conditions – overall trajectory of Iran’s enrichment programme and JCPOA diplomacy going into spring 2026.  
Base rate – historical frequency with which IAEA Iran reports (esp. the February or March Board of Governors report) have mentioned new cascades.  
Resolution mechanism – typical publication dates and document codes (GOV/202X/XX, GOV/INF/..) so forecasters know when/where to look.  
Key drivers – recent Iranian announcements, construction activity at Natanz/Fordow, and inspection access issues.  
Current state – the most recent IAEA report (likely GOV/2026/xx of late-Feb 2026) plus any press leaks or satellite imagery after that report.  
Contrarian check – evidence Iran may pause or roll back installation (e.g., a political deal) or that the IAEA cannot confirm installations due to lack of access.
