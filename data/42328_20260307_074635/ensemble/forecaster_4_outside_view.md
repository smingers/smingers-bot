(d) Justification

The outside view should start from broad institutional reality: in most democracies, replacing more than two ministers within a 73-day window is uncommon. If we used the full Fifth Republic as the reference class, the probability would likely be fairly low.

But the broad class is a poor fit. The better comparison is France under Macron’s second term after the loss of an absolute majority and especially after the 2024 dissolution and prolonged fragmentation. The source packet consistently describes:
- repeated government turnover,
- unusually short-lived prime ministers,
- frequent cabinet reconfigurations,
- and a record number of distinct ministerial officeholders.

The strongest numeric anchor is seven governments and 130 ministerial officeholders since May 2022. That indicates an environment where cabinet instability is much higher than normal, and where crossing the threshold of “more than two ministers replaced” can happen via:
1. a full or near-full government recomposition,
2. a medium-sized reshuffle to respond to parliamentary or coalition stress,
3. piecemeal decrees across multiple dates that aggregate to >2 by the resolution rules.

The threshold itself is modest. This is not asking about a wholesale government collapse. More than two replacements can be reached through a fairly ordinary corrective reshuffle or political bargain. That materially raises the baseline relative to asking about a PM resignation.

On the other hand, there are reasons not to go too high:
- Even in unstable periods, large changes are episodic, not constant.
- The window is only about 10 weeks.
- Some instability manifests as parliamentary drama without ministerial personnel changes.
- The status quo in many short windows is “no major change.”

So the outside view should be above a generic historical baseline, well above 20%, but below even odds unless we had direct contemporaneous evidence of an imminent censure crisis or cabinet bargain. Since this is outside view only, I should avoid overreacting to the dramatic tone of the sources.

- Generic Fifth Republic short-window rate: probably low, perhaps around 10–20%.
- Crisis-era Macron second-term short-window rate: likely materially higher, perhaps around 30–45%.
- Adjusting for the modest threshold (>2 replacements) but still short time remaining, the best outside-view estimate is in the high 30s.
