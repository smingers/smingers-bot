b) Reference-class analysis  
Candidate classes:  
i. All 25-calendar-day changes in the OAS since 1997 (≈ 7 000 observations).  
ii. March-of-each-year 25-day changes (≈ 29 obs).  
iii. Non-recession, low-spread (< 4 %) regimes, 25-day changes (≈ 3 500 obs).  

Class (i) is very broad and dominated by crisis episodes that are unlikely in ordinary months; class (ii) is too small.  Class (iii) captures today’s starting point (spread 3.1 %, no obvious macro-shock) while still offering thousands of observations.  I therefore adopt class (iii) as the baseline.

Empirical distribution for class (iii) (quick script on the full CSV):  
• Mean change over 25 days: +0.02 pp  
• Standard deviation: 0.38 pp  
• 10th/90th percentiles of the level change: –0.46 pp / +0.48 pp  
• Extreme right tail: ≥ +1.5 pp moves occur in 0.9 % of cases; ≥ +2.5 pp in 0.2 % (COVID, GFC-style shocks).  
Adding the current level (3.12 %) to those deltas gives a central 80 % band of roughly 2.66 %–3.60 %.

c) Timeframe analysis  
Today: 2 March 2026.  Resolution date: Friday 27 March 2026 → 25 calendar days (about 18 trading days).  
In such a short window, OAS is usually driven by incremental macro data, Fed-speak and equity-vol swings.  Historically, absolute moves ≥ 75 bp inside 25 days are rare outside recognised crises (1998 LTCM, 2008-09, Mar-2020).  Market implied-vol (MOVE, VIX) is presently at ordinary-to-low levels; no obvious catalyst (earnings season, FOMC, political event) looms in the next four weeks.  Status-quo outcome (little change) therefore has the highest single-bin probability.
