(b) Reference-class analysis  
Candidate reference classes:  
1. All 10-calendar-day percentage changes in USD/EUR since Jan-1999 (≈6500 observations).  
2. Same but limited to the post-Covid regime (Jan-2021 onward).  
3. A “policy event window” class (10-day moves centred on ECB or Fed meetings).  
Class 1 is broad, has the most data, and already “contains” classes 2 and 3; it is therefore adopted for the outside view.  Historical volatility has been broadly stable, so regime bias is modest.

(c) Timeframe analysis  
Forecast horizon = 10 calendar days (02 Mar → 12 Mar 2026).  From the Agent’s methodology note and market data:  
• Empirical daily σ ≈ 0.46 %.  
• σ10 ≈ √10 × 0.46 % ≈ 1.5 %.  
• Historical 10-day return distribution is close to normal with fat tails: about 16 % of 10-day moves exceed ±1.5 %, 5 % exceed ±3 %.  Extreme 10-day swings > ±5 % have occurred only during the 2008 and 2020 crises (prob. < 0.5 %).  
Important scheduled events inside the window: ECB meeting (5 Mar), US payrolls (6 Mar).  Such events can lift realised vol but rarely beyond the 95th percentile of the long-run distribution.

(d) Justification  
Status quo (do-nothing) forecast is the latest published fixing: 1.178.  Drift over 10 days is statistically negligible relative to volatility, although very recent momentum is mildly upward.  Combining:  
• Central tendency anchored near 1.18.  
• 1-σ band (≈ ±1.5 %) → 1.160–1.196 defines the middle 68 %.  
• To allow for tail risk from the ECB/Fed narrative, widen the 10th/90th percentiles to roughly ±2.8 % (historical 95 % band).  That pushes the 10th to ~1.145 and the 90th to ~1.210.  
• Slight positive skew included because the Fed is expected to sound more dovish than the ECB during the period (jobs report + no immediate Fed meeting), giving the euro a small upside tilt.

Outside view calibration:
Baseline distribution ≈ Log-normal with μ ≈ ln(1.178) and σln ≈ 0.015.  Percentiles drawn from that curve are then nudged +0.000–0.003 higher to reflect the modest bullish fundamental bias, but no percentile is shifted by more than 0.25 % of spot, keeping this an “outside-view” rather than an inside-view forecast.  Tails (10th/90th) are set wider than ±2 σ to remain well-calibrated to historical upset frequency (≈5 % beyond ±3 %).  All values are rounded to the nearest 0.0005.
