## Summary: "Ethel Kennedy was social activist who championed human rights after RFK's death" — Art Threat (Feb. 27, 2026)

**Author:** Alexander Woodward | **Source:** Art Threat

---

### Key Biographical Facts

- Ethel Skakel Kennedy was born into a wealthy Chicago family; her father founded Great Lakes Carbon Corporation.
- She met Robert F. Kennedy in 1945 at Manhattanville College and married him in 1950.
- The couple had **11 children** between 1951 and 1968 and lived at Hickory Hill, a Virginia estate that served as a hub for intellectuals and politicians.

### Tragedy and Activism

- RFK was assassinated on **June 5, 1968**, at the Ambassador Hotel in Los Angeles. Ethel was present and three months pregnant with their youngest child, Rory.
- According to biographer **Evan Thomas**, Ethel vowed never to remarry and instead dedicated herself to furthering Robert's legacy.
- Within months of RFK's death, she founded what is now **Robert F. Kennedy Human Rights**, focusing on gun control, environmental protection, mental health, and international human rights.
- As late as **March 2016 (age 88)**, she marched with farmworkers demanding wage increases.

### Recognition and Awards

- **1981:** Received the Robert F. Kennedy Medal from President Reagan.
- **2014:** Awarded the **Presidential Medal of Freedom** by President Obama.
- A bridge over the Anacostia River in Washington, D.C., was renamed the **Ethel Kennedy Bridge** in 2014.

### Death and Memorial

- Ethel Kennedy **died on October 10, 2024**, in Boston following a stroke.
- Her memorial service on **October 16, 2024**, at the Cathedral of Saint Matthew the Apostle was attended by President Biden and former presidents Obama and Clinton, who delivered eulogies. Artist **Sting** performed at the service.
- She was **buried at Arlington National Cemetery** beside Robert F. Kennedy.

### Broader Legacy

- The RFK Human Rights organization eventually rebranded to honor both Robert and Ethel Kennedy.
- Notable children include **Kathleen Kennedy Townsend** (Maryland Lt. Governor), **Joseph P. Kennedy II** (U.S. Congressman), and **Robert F. Kennedy Jr.**
- Taylor Swift reportedly drew inspiration from Ethel's love story for her song *Starlight*.
