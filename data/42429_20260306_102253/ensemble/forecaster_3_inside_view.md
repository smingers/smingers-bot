(d) Justification

Start from the outside view: 37 / 36 / 27.

Why move away from that baseline at all?
Because the reference class was built from ordinary recent 8-day windows, whereas current conditions include an extraordinary, highly salient new interstate war involving the US, Israel, and Iran, plus the reported killing of Iran’s supreme leader. That is not just another noisy week for the term “war.” The current inside view is therefore meaningfully less analogous to the average recent window.

How far should that move go?
Not too far, for three reasons:
1. Mar 6 already comes after the initial shock and after a visible cooling from the Feb 28 peak.  
2. Trends is relative and noisy; a lot of headline intensity may still not translate to a >3 net move.  
3. Public attention often decays quickly after the first days of a crisis unless there is a new dramatic development.

So the inside-view push is mainly:
- Up a bit for Increases, because the conflict is active and could easily produce fresh shocks before Mar 14.
- Slightly down or roughly flat for Doesn’t change, because despite methodological stickiness, current volatility is high.
- Slightly down from the outside-view decrease probability, because the war is ongoing rather than clearly fading into routine coverage.

My net adjustment:
- Increases: up from 37 to 41
- Doesn’t change: down slightly from 36 to 33
- Decreases: down slightly from 27 to 26

This is not a huge shift, because although the inside view is compelling, Mar 6 is not pre-war; it is already during high attention. The key question is not “will war searches be high?” but “will Mar 14 be more than 3 points above or below Mar 6?” That comparative framing limits confidence.
