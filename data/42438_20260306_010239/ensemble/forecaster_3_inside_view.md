## (d) Justification

The outside view already gave Duke a strong profile: most likely outcomes clustered in Elite Eight / Final Four, with about 24% combined for making the title game and 20% winning it all. The key question is whether current inside-view evidence justifies meaningful movement.

### Status quo if current trend continues
If Duke continues roughly as it has through early March:
- It gets a No. 1 seed, probably the overall No. 1.
- It likely opens against a very weak 16 and then an 8/9 opponent.
- It enters the tournament as one of perhaps 3–5 true title contenders.

Under that status quo, Duke should be **very likely** to reach at least the Sweet 16, **more likely than not** to reach the Elite Eight, and a **serious but still minority** chance to win the championship.

### Why not move much higher?
Because the outside view is already conditioned on “elite likely 1-seed.” To justify a large further increase, we’d need circumstances making the historical 1-seed reference class less relevant—e.g., a historically dominant team with overwhelming market pricing, or a field that has collapsed due to injuries. We do not have that.

In fact, current evidence cuts both ways:
- Duke is elite by both resume and efficiency.
- But Michigan, Arizona, UConn, Florida, Houston and others still exist as plausible title threats.
- Betting markets and bracketology do not depict Duke as remotely invulnerable.

So the right inside-view move is a **modest positive adjustment**, not a dramatic one.

### Where to shift probability
The strongest evidence reduces the tail risk of early exits slightly:
- Round of 64 should stay around 1.
- Round of 32 maybe slips below the 8 outside-view number.

The most natural receiving bins are:
- **Elite Eight**
- **Final Four**
- a modest bump to **championship win/loss**

But title-win should not rise too much because six straight wins remain hard, and top-team parity appears meaningful.

My updated thinking:
- Duke is a bit more likely than the outside view to get through the first weekend.
- A modest amount of mass should move from Round of 32/Sweet 16 into Elite Eight/Final Four.
- Title-win can increase a little, but not into the mid-20s absent stronger market support.
