(b)  Reference-class analysis  
Possible classes:  
1. All tournament teams since 1985 – too coarse (base rate of cutting down nets is 1.5%).  
2. All No. 1 seeds since 1985 (160 cases) – captures bracket position similar to what Duke is overwhelmingly likely to have in 2026.  
3. Duke’s own tournament history post-1985 (36 appearances) – adjusts for school-specific coaching/recruiting culture.  
4. “Top overall” No. 1 seeds (committee’s top line, 40 cases) – very small N, performance actually slightly worse than generic No. 1 seeds.

I choose a weighted blend of class 2 (70% weight) and class 3 (30%) to incorporate Duke’s strong historical out-performance without leaning on this specific roster (that would be an inside-view move).

(c)  Timeframe analysis
