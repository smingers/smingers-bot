Only one precedent (1989): Khamenei selected within ~1 day as acting leader, permanent within ~2 months. This suggests rapid selection is possible. However, 1989 had a clear kingmaker (Rafsanjani) and a pre-positioned candidate. The current situation is more contested.

Examples: Saudi Arabia (smooth succession within royal family), Vatican (conclave process), North Korea (hereditary). These suggest institutions tend to preserve themselves and select quickly, but the specific dynamics vary greatly.
