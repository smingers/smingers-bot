Across sources, the clear factual overlap—IRGC coercion, Assembly disrupted, Mojtaba frontrunner—comes from at least three independent, name-brand outlets (Reuters, Guardian, NYT).

2. Evidence analysis
Strong evidence
• Structural: 88-member, hard-line dominated Assembly must pick a single leader (unchanged since 1989).  
• IRGC preference for Mojtaba corroborated by Reuters, NYT, Liberty Times.  
Moderate evidence
• Reports of a vote already occurring (Iran International, NY Post) – unconfirmed.  
• Trump’s public opposition – may raise assassination risk, but foreign pressure historically has limited sway over Assembly choices.  
Weak evidence
• Speculation that Mojtaba is dead; no proof and later reports place him alive.

Weighting: strong evidence shifts probability toward Mojtaba; moderate/weak evidence tempers with risk of push-back and mortality.

3. Timeframe analysis
301 days until 1 Jan 27. Plenty of time for (a) Assembly to formalise a leader, (b) that leader to be killed or overthrown. Halving the window (≈5 months) would reduce “None” ~3 pts and raise Mojtaba (if already seated) by ~3 pts; doubling (≈20 months) would increase the assassination/abolition tail by ~4 pts, mostly at the expense of the leading individual.

4. Justification – key factors moving the outside view
a) Multiple reliable reports that the IRGC is openly pressuring—or has already induced—the Assembly to install Mojtaba (strong evidence) → +11 pts to Mojtaba vs baseline.  
b) Eight members refusing to attend shows non-trivial clerical resistance (moderate) → keeps Mojtaba well below 50 %.  
c) Wartime security crisis historically drives selectorates toward continuity with the security apparatus, boosting Mojtaba & Mohseni-Eje’i, trimming reformist prospects (Hassan Khomeini –2 pts).  
d) Heightened assassination threat declared by Israel/US introduces ~30 % conditional mortality for whomever is chosen, inflating “None” and “Someone else” buckets.  
e) Interim trio legally unlimited in duration but precedent (1989) and regime self-interest make a 10-month vacancy unlikely → keeps “None” at mid-teens, not majority.
