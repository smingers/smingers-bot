(b)  Reference class analysis  
Candidate-specific forecasting is rare, but two broad reference classes exist:  
RC-1:  “Supreme-leader style theocracies” (Iran 1989 only).  Too small.  
RC-2:  “Autocratic successions in single-party or revolutionary regimes since 1945 where a quasi-religious/ideological post wields ultimate power” (e.g., China 1976, 1997; North Korea 1994, 2011; Cuba 2006; Vietnam 1986).  Sizeable (≈15 cases).  In 12/15 (≈80 %) the successor emerged from the inner circle of security-bureaucratic elites but was not a direct family member; in 3/15 (≈20 %—both North Koreas and Syria 2000) the heir was a son.  Given Iran’s clerical-collective structure and public discomfort with dynasticism, RC-2 is the more informative baseline.
