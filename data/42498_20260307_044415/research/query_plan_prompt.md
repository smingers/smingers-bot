Under [10 U.S.C. § 3252](https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/3252), the U.S. Secretary of Defense, of the Army, of the Navy, or of the Air Force may exclude certain "sources" from competing for government contracts (or subcontracts) for so-called [national security systems](https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/44/3552#b_6) in order to reduce "supply chain risk." Separately, under [41 U.S.C § 4713](https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/41/4713), the head of an executive agency may exclude a "source" from competing for government contacts for certain information technology or telecommunications systems, in order to reduce supply chain risk. Similarly here, supply chain risk is defined to mean the risk that a person may sabotage or manipulate the system(s) in question. By law, a party excluded under this statute [may petition for judicial review](https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/41/1327).
