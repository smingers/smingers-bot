Because even substantial geopolitical volatility often decays over weeks if it does not morph into a durable macro shock. Historical VIX behavior is mean-reverting, and 25+ usually requires either:
- active crisis escalation,
- a sharp equity drawdown,
- systemic funding stress,
- or a major policy surprise.

We have a serious geopolitical shock, but not yet evidence of broad financial-system stress. The current VIX is elevated, not panicky.

### Why not move sharply downward into 15-20?
Because:
- current VIX is already ~23.75,
- the shock is fresh, not resolved,
- oil/inflation uncertainty could linger into April,
- and late April earnings season tends to keep hedging demand alive.

A return to 15-20 is plausible if the conflict de-escalates and markets stabilize, but it no longer looks as attractive as in a normal soft-landing environment.
