2. Base rate / historical context

• In the 2025 calendar year crude-oil and equity futures delivered very different volatility profiles: CL experienced several ±10 % fortnights, while ES rarely moved more than ±4 % in a comparable span.²  
• CL has historically shown heightened sensitivity to unexpected EIA inventory swings and to unplanned OPEC+/geopolitical shocks; ES tends to react more to U.S. macro data (CPI, PCE) and FOMC meetings.  
• When major Fed events have coincided with elevated energy-sector geopolitical tension (e.g., Sept 2019 drone strike on Abqaiq, Feb 2022 Russia–Ukraine invasion), relative performance between CL and ES has been wide (often >±10 pp) but directionally varied, depending on whether risk-off flows or inflation hedging dominated.²
