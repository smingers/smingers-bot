PART 1: Key uncertainties

1. Current trend position: Is the Mar 4 value elevated, depressed, or typical relative to the last few weeks/months?  
   • Matters because a reversion-to-mean pattern would bias the 10-day change toward the opposite direction of whatever today’s level is.

2. Cause of the recent 74 spike: Was that number driven by a specific news item (e.g., illness rumour, documentary release, political mention) that has already faded or might keep echoing?  
   • If the driver has passed, interest is likely to fall; if follow-up coverage is scheduled, it may stay high or rise further.

3. Probability of a major fresh catalyst within the next 10 days, foremost Ethel Kennedy’s possible death or major hospitalization.  
   • A death would almost certainly push the score well above +3; without such an event, large movements are rare.

4. Scheduled or predictable media events between Mar 4–14 that feature Ethel Kennedy (anniversary specials, film releases, RFK Jr. campaign appearances mentioning her, etc.).  
   • Known future coverage could push interest higher even without unforeseen events.

5. Typical day-to-day volatility for this search term at daily resolution.  
   • Knowing the standard deviation of daily moves helps judge whether a ±3 change can occur from noise alone.
