## 7. Recommendations

### Research Improvements

- **Improve AskNews query specificity:** The current query was too broad ("Will PCG's market close price on 2026-02-13 be higher than its market close price on 2026-02-04?"). A more targeted query like "PG&E PCG earnings February 2026 analyst expectations guidance" would return more relevant results.
- **Add a broader market context query:** Include a query for S&P 500 or utilities sector (XLU) performance trends and upcoming macro events (Fed meetings, CPI releases, VIX levels) that could affect the stock during the forecast window.
- **Surface recent options flow/positioning data:** For short-term stock price questions, options positioning and unusual activity data would be more predictive than 12-month analyst price targets.

### Prompt/Pipeline Improvements

- **Add guidance on appropriate adjustment magnitudes for short-term stock price questions:** The inside view prompt could include a note that analyst 12-month price targets and long-term valuation gaps have minimal predictive power for 1-2 week stock returns, and that adjustments from the base rate should be commensurately small (typically 1-5pp for a single factor, not 8-10pp).
- **Clarify timeframe sensitivity instructions:** The prompt asks forecasters to describe how the prediction changes if the window is halved or doubled. Adding guidance that excluding/including a major catalyst (like earnings) should primarily affect uncertainty, not necessarily direction, would prevent errors like S2-1's "65-70% if halved" claim.

### Model-Specific Feedback

- **Sonnet 4.5:** Tends to over-weight narrative factors (analyst sentiment, momentum stories) relative to their empirical predictive power for short-term stock prices. Consider adding a calibration note in the prompt specifically for stock price questions that emphasizes the efficient market hypothesis and random-walk base rates.
- **GPT-5.2:** Excellent disciplined outside-view reasoning. Its restrained updates (48% to 51%) are well-suited to questions where the base rate is near 50%. No significant improvements needed.
- **o3:** Consistently strong quantitative approach. The parametric normal distribution calculations add rigor. One minor suggestion: when providing rolling-window empirical frequencies (e.g., "25/51 = 49%"), explicitly noting the confidence interval around this estimate would further strengthen the analysis.
