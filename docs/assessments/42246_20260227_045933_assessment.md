- On the **current side**, the Google News query on “H.R. 33 status / Senate companion” and AskNews query on the exact act name and 2026 prospects are tightly targeted; they pull in coverage that explicitly mentions House passage, Senate consideration, and hoped‑for passage by 2026.

## 7. Overall Assessment

### Strengths

1. **Query design is conceptually strong:** It directly targets the **correct reference class and the exact bill**, asking for enactment history of similar foreign double‑tax‑relief bills and current status/coverage of H.R. 33.  
2. **Best sources are genuinely on‑point:** The Agent base‑rate report and the news pieces about House passage and U.S.–Taiwan double‑tax negotiations are **highly relevant** and appropriately used as anchors.  
3. **Inside view treats noisy articles cautiously:** Forecasters do not appear to over‑react to tangential Taiwan macro/defense/tariff stories; they treat them mostly as background rather than as decisive evidence about this bill’s short‑run enactment odds.

### Weaknesses

1. **Congress.gov failure not cleanly backfilled:** After 403s and extraction errors on the primary resolution sources, the pipeline did not deliberately switch to a clean status surrogate (e.g., scraping a public bill‑tracker that’s accessible), leaving a gap in hard procedural detail.  
2. **AskNews retrieval is too broad:** A single, high‑recall query pulled in many Taiwan‑related but **question‑irrelevant** articles (housing vacancy taxes, stock‑market performance, general trade‑policy noise), which the summarizer had to wade through; this reduces efficiency and risks subtle over‑weighting of context that doesn’t actually bear on the bill’s near‑term passage.  
3. **Some historical Google hits are only weakly diagnostic:** Generic essays on congressional productivity and “legislative theater” are less informative than, say, targeted stats on tax‑legislation enactment or Finance‑reported bills, and they occupy space that could have held more tailored base‑rate data.

## 8. Recommendations

### Research Improvements

- Tighten AskNews queries to **explicitly require mention of the bill or a double‑tax avoidance agreement**, e.g. include “double taxation relief”, “H.R. 33”, or “tax treaty” in the query, to avoid flooding the context with broadly Taiwan‑related but question‑irrelevant stories.  
- Add a **fallback status‑source strategy**: when Congress.gov is blocked, automatically query an accessible bill‑tracker (e.g., GovTrack or official press releases) and label it clearly as the surrogate primary source for status.

### Prompt/Pipeline Improvements

- In the research planner prompt, explicitly instruct: “Prefer sources that **directly mention the specific bill number or act name** over general Taiwan geopolitical or economic coverage when assembling context.”  
- In the summarization step, explicitly mark each article as **“directly about the bill / about U.S.–Taiwan double‑tax agreement” vs. “broader context only”**, so forecasters can more easily down‑weight the latter.

### Model-Specific Feedback

- Forecasters generally did a good job of **focusing on the bill‑relevant subset** of the AskNews bundle, but there is room to be even more explicit in Step 1/Step 2 about **which cited facts come from directly relevant articles** versus general background pieces.
