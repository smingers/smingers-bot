#### yFinance: Should it have been used, and was it?

- **Should it have been used?**  
  Yes. This is a **numeric question about a traded index (CBOE VIX)** where the target is a function of the **distribution of maximum intraday highs over rolling 10-trading-day windows**. A structured price API like yFinance can:
  - Pull a long history of VIX OHLC data.
  - Compute empirical 10-day-window maxima and their percentile distribution.
  - Provide concrete analogues for war/oil-shock periods vs. calm periods.
  This fits squarely under “If stocks/securities” in the template and would materially improve calibration of both central estimates and tails.

- **Was it used?**  
  - **Main research planner:** No. `search_research_plan.json` shows historical tools limited to **Google**; `centralized_research.historical.tools_used` is `["Google"]`. The assembled historical context contains descriptive pages about where VIX data can be downloaded, but no evidence of a yFinance-driven time-series pull or 10-day-window analysis.  
  - **Supervisor:** Yes, but narrowly. The supervisor’s Agent report (`supervisor_research.md`) explicitly cites **“(yFinance)”** for the March 2, 2026 VIX close (20.44) and intraday high (25.24). This is a **single-date lookup**, not a base-rate distribution.

**Assessment:** For this question, yFinance (or an equivalent structured VIX history source) **should have been used in the core research phase to compute historical distributions of 10-day maximum intraday highs**, but it was only touched late and narrowly by the supervisor to pin down the March 2 starting level. That underuse is a meaningful but not catastrophic research gap.

## 8. Recommendations

### Research Improvements

- Add a dedicated **VIX history module** (likely wrapping yFinance or an internal database) that:
  - Pulls long-run OHLC VIX data.
  - Computes rolling 10-trading-day maximum intraday highs.
  - Produces base-rate percentiles for calm vs. crisis regimes.
- Surface those statistics directly in prompts (S1 and supervisor) so forecasters can explicitly reference, e.g., “Historically, starting from VIX≈20, the 10-day max exceeds 40 in X% of windows, 50 in Y%, 60 in Z%.”

### Prompt/Pipeline Improvements

- For **numeric maximum-value questions**, adjust prompts to ask:
  - How daily behaviour maps to **window-max distributions**.
  - How much of a large spike is likely to occur *before* vs. *during* the specified window.
- Encourage Step 2 to be explicit about which parts of the distribution (P10, P50, P90) are anchored in **data vs. judgment**, especially under conflict scenarios.
