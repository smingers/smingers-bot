# Research Brief: What will U.S. Customs and Border Protection report as the number of Southwest Land Border Encounters in March 2026?

RESEARCH BRIEF  
Question: What will U.S. Customs and Border Protection report as the number of Southwest Land Border Encounters in March 2026?  
(Statistics are those found in the research trace only. “Southwest Land Border Encounters” = U.S. Border Patrol Title 8 apprehensions + Office of Field Operations Title 8 inadmissibles at SW land ports + any Title 42 expulsions before May 2023.)

--------------------------------------------------------------------
1. Key findings  

A. Latest official monthly numbers found  
• January 2026 – 9,726 Southwest land encounters (CBP news release, 24 Jan 2026). 6,070 of these were Border Patrol apprehensions between ports (NewsNation, 20 Feb 2026).  
• December 2025 – 6,478 Border Patrol apprehensions at the SW border and 30,698 total nationwide encounters; DHS press release claims “92 % below Biden-era peak.” (PJ Media & Epoch Times summaries of 16-17 Jan 2026 DHS release).  
• October 2025 – 9,845 Border Patrol apprehensions at the SW border; 30,561 total nationwide encounters (DHS Oct-2025 release).  
• March 2025 – 11,017 Southwest land encounters (Good Judgment Open article citing CBP data).  
• February 2025 – 11,709 Southwest land encounters (CBP “February 2025 Monthly Update”).  

B. Annual context  
• FY 2025 (Oct 2024–Sep 2025) – 237,538 encounters at the SW border, lowest fiscal-year total since 1970 (Pew Research Center, 2 Feb 2026, citing CBP).  
• FY 2024 – >1.5 million encounters; FY 2023 – >2 million; FY 2022 – 2.2 million (Pew, 2 Feb 2026).  
• Since Feb 2025, monthly encounters have stayed “below 10,000” (Pew quoting CBP monthly data).

C. Policy/environment signals relevant to March 2026  
• DHS/CBP repeatedly highlight “zero releases” of inadmissible migrants for 8–10 consecutive months through January 2026 (PJ Media 17 Jan 2026; DHS/CBP releases).  
• Secretary of Homeland Security Kristi Noem reports a 96 % drop in daily encounters compared with Biden-era averages (Senate testimony covered by multiple outlets, 3-4 Mar 2026).  
• Border infrastructure expansion (e.g., “Smart Wall”) and Operation River Wall announced in Oct 2025; DHS claims these are driving lower encounters (DHS release 3 Feb 2026).  
• Partial DHS funding lapse in March 2026 may affect operations, but TSA/CBP staff are still working without pay (Men’s Journal, 3 Mar 2026).

--------------------------------------------------------------------
2. Base rate / historical context  

Monthly Southwest Land Border Encounters (selected):  
• March 2024 – 189,359 (Good Judgment Open article citing CBP).  
• March 2023 – >200 k (context from Baker Institute article, early 2026).  
• March 2025 – 11,017 (CBP).  
Thus, March 2025 set a new floor roughly 94 % below the prior-year March.  

Trend since Trump’s second inauguration (20 Jan 2025):  
– Rapid collapse from ~242 k encounters in Jan 2024 to single-digit-thousands by mid-2025.  
– FY-to-FY drop from >1.5 M (FY 2024) to 0.238 M (FY 2025).  
– Encounter levels have remained in the 6-12 k range per month through at least January 2026 for Border Patrol apprehensions; combined (USBP + OFO) totals appear to run 9-12 k.  

Historically, before Title 42 ended (Mar 2020-May 2023), encounters included large numbers of immediate expulsions; after May 2023 Title 8 procedures dominate.

--------------------------------------------------------------------
3. Current situation (as of 5 March 2026)  

• No official February 2026 Southwest land total has yet been posted on CBP’s data portal or cited in press coverage; only qualitative references to “continued historic lows.”  
• No preliminary March 2026 daily-average figures have surfaced in media, congressional testimony, or DHS statements reviewed.  
• CBP and DHS messaging continues to emphasize:  
  – 8-10 straight months of “zero releases/parole.”  
  – Daily apprehensions < 250 and 95-96 % below Biden-era daily average.  
• Political/legal headwinds: DHS budget still un-passed; Senate hearings focusing on DHS use-of-force incidents; watchdog lawsuits for transparency. No indication these have yet altered field enforcement posture.  

--------------------------------------------------------------------
4. Key uncertainties  

1. February 2026 data gap – Without the official February figure, it is unclear whether the downward trend accelerated, plateaued, or reversed immediately before March.  
2. No March mid-month indicators – Unlike earlier periods when leaks or DHS press calls sometimes offered daily averages, nothing similar is available for March 2026 to date.  
3. Impact of DHS partial shutdown – Unknown operational or reporting disruptions that could affect March encounter numbers (e.g., staffing, data lag).  
4. Potential external shocks – Escalating U.S.–Iran tensions, cartel violence, or policy changes could rapidly change flows; no quantitative measure yet.  
5. Data consistency – Some press releases mix nationwide and Southwest statistics, making precise SW land trend extraction difficult.
