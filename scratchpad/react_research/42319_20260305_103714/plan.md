# Research Plan: What will U.S. Customs and Border Protection report as the number of Southwest Land Border Encounters in March 2026?

PART 1: Key uncertainties

1. Partial March-2026 encounter counts already circulating  
   Why it matters:  If DHS/CBP officials, members of Congress, or journalists have quoted “so-far-this-month” or “daily average” figures, these provide the single best anchor for the final March total.

2. Underlying trajectory from October 2025-February 2026  
   Why it matters: The most recent five-month trend tells us whether flows have been rising, falling, or flat going into March and supplies the baseline for extrapolating the month’s remainder if no new information emerges.

3. Effect of very recent U.S. or Mexican policy moves (Jan–Mar 2026)  
   Why it matters: Emergency asylum limits, Mexico’s interior checkpoints, the end of humanitarian parole programs, Texas state actions, etc., can shift encounters tens of thousands within weeks. Knowing which measures are in force and when they started is crucial for adjusting the baseline.

4. Composition and origin-country pressures driving surges (e.g., Venezuelans, Haitians)  
   Why it matters: Different nationalities respond differently to policy (Cubans vs. Mexicans, for example). Sharp changes in origin mix—prompted by economic or political shocks abroad—can quickly move the headline number.
