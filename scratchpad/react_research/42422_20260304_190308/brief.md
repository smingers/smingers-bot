# Research Brief: Will the interest in “ethel kennedy” change between 2026-03-04 and 2026-03-14 according to Google Trends?

Research Brief  
Topic: Will U.S. Google-Trends interest in “ethel kennedy” (daily series) move by more than ±3 points between 4 Mar 2026 and 14 Mar 2026?

----------------------------------------------------------------  
Key findings  
1. Current Google-Trends level & recent volatility  
   • Daily value for 4 Mar 2026 = 26 (GoogleTrendsData, Step 1).  
   • 90-day mean = 3.9; standard deviation = 14.9 (ibid.).  
   • Exceptional spike: 65 on 27 Feb and 100 on 28 Feb, then rapid decay to 41 (2 Mar), 28 (3 Mar), 26 (4 Mar).  

2. Empirical 10-day change distribution (90-day look-back)  
   • In 93 % of 10-day windows, absolute change ≤ 3 points (“Doesn’t change”).  
   • In 7 % of windows, value increased by > 3.  
   • No windows saw a decrease > 3 (GoogleTrendsData, Step 1).  

3. Cause of the late-Feb spike  
   • Episode 5 (“Battery Park”) of FX/Hulu limited series “Love Story: John F. Kennedy Jr. & Carolyn Bessette” aired 26 Feb 2026 and centred on Ethel Kennedy’s intimidating dinner with Carolyn (IMDb news recap; Decider & Vulture episode recaps, Steps 3 & 6).  
   • Multiple outlets on 27 Feb–3 Mar highlighted Jessica Harper’s “chilling” portrayal of Ethel as “undisputed matriarch” (TV Insider, Gold Derby via IMDb; Decider; Vulture, Steps 3 & 6).  

4. Forward media schedule relevant to the forecast window  
   • “Love Story” is a 9-episode season releasing weekly on Thursdays (FX 9 p.m. ET / Hulu next-day). (Decider, Newsweek, Town & Country, Step 4)  
        – Episode 6 “The Wedding”: airs 5 Mar 2026 (one day after the starting date).  
        – Episode 7 “Obsession”: airs 12 Mar 2026 (inside window).  
   • No synopsis yet specifies Ethel’s screen time for Ep 6/7; however, wedding and family-conflict themes imply possible re-appearance. (No direct mention, Step 7).  

5. Lack of longer-term context  
   • 12-month Google-Trends pull returned “No data available” (Step 5), so prior large spikes and decay patterns cannot be quantified beyond the last 90 days.  

----------------------------------------------------------------  
Base rate / historical context  
• Within the last 90 days, low-salience queries for “Ethel Kennedy” almost always hover 0–2, punctuated by a single media-driven surge.  
• Given the empirical distribution above, a > 3-point move over any 10-day span happens about once in 14 windows (≈7 %).  
• When a surge does occur, decay from peak 100 to 26 took four days (27 Feb–4 Mar), showing that elevated interest can subside quickly.  
• No prior data to judge recurrence frequency of TV-driven spikes beyond this single instance.  

----------------------------------------------------------------  
Current situation (as of 4 Mar 2026 UTC)
